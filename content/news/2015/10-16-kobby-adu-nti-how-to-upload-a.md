<p>As best practice, we advise our customers to use the <a title="" _jive_internal="true" href="/community?id=community_question&sys_id=8dda0b6ddb5cdbc01dcaf3231f9619bd">system fonts provided with an instance</a>. However, for those who do not wish to be limited with the out-of-the-box offerings there is a workaround. <span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">The <a title="ki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Legacy:Uploading_a_File#gsc.tab=0" href="http://wiki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Legacy:Uploading_a_File#gsc.tab=0">Upload File module is obsolete in Fuji</a> denying you the rights to upload to the /scs directory. This catches many people offguard especially those following the instructions from <a title="w.servicenowelite.com/blog/2014/4/22/servicenow-cms-fonts" href="http://www.servicenowelite.com/blog/2014/4/22/servicenow-cms-fonts">ServiceNow CMS fonts by ServiceNow Elite</a> by <a __default_attr="2697" __jive_macro_name="user" class="jive_macro_user jive_macro" data-orig-content="Mike Kaufman. " href="/community?id=community_user_profile&user=f99f062ddb181fc09c9ffb651f96193f" modifiedtitle="true" title="Mike Kaufman. ">Mike Kaufman. </a></span></p><p></p><p>You can utilize the <strong>[sys_attachment] table</strong> to put font files into CMS. See <a href="http://wiki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Uploading_Attachments#gsc.tab=0" style="font-size: 13.3333330154419px;" title="http://wiki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Uploading_Attachments#gsc.tab=0">Uploading Attachments - ServiceNow Wiki</a> to understand how attachments work.</p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">In the following example I have attached the font Lato-Regular to an incident record</span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"><img   alt="image 1 PM.JPG" class="image-0 jive-image" src="6c435dcadb10d7041dcaf3231f961900.iix" style="height: 276px; width: 620px;"/><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">Once the file has been uploaded you should be able to access it by navigating to <em>&lt;your_instance_name&gt;.service-now.com/sys_attachment_list.do, o</em>r typing <em>sys_attachment.list</em> in the navigator. Sort the records by date or locate the file name</span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"><img   alt="image 2 PM.JPG" class="image-1 jive-image" src="afa2ff35dbdc1fc03eb27a9e0f961997.iix" style="height: 99px; width: 620px;"/><br/></span></p><p></p><p></p><ol><li><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">Double click on the </span><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"><em>Table sys ID</em></span><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"> column on the record and copy the value. You will need this to </span>reference<span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"> the font file</span></li><li>Use the Navigator to traverse to <strong>Content Management &gt; Default CSS - Base Styles</strong>.</li><li>Add a @font-face rule to the style sheet you are using for your CMS portal. Ensure you reference the URI location of the font in the attachment table</li></ol><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">       </span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"><img   alt="image 2015-10-18 at 12.56.26 AM.JPG" class="jive-image image-3" src="5767a3b9dbd41fc03eb27a9e0f9619b4.iix" style="height: 213px; width: 620px;"/><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">   4.   Do not forget to set the <em>font-family</em> key value pair where you require in your style sheet </span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt;
