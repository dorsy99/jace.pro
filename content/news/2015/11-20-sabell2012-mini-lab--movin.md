<p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Have you ever made modifications to your instance only to realize that those mods were being placed into the Default update set and not into the one you thought the changes were supposed to go to?   Has it caused you to beat your fists on your keyboard until the keys fly off and you spend an hour searching around all the books and clutter on your office floor retrieving them?   Has it caused you to seek out a psychoanalyst?   Well!   Help is here!</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">To begin with you could move your changes using the normal method of simply editing up the Customer Change record and specifying the update set you want to be in.   While this is great for one or two updates; it is totally untenable for more than that.   I have zero desire to spend an afternoon moving all those records!</span></p><p></p><p><img  __jive_id="45416" class="image-5 jive-image" src="9551448adb141344e9737a9e0f961994.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 392px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">However, there is a way of mass moving change records, but you have to know where to look, and what you are about. </span></p><p></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 14pt;"><strong>Lab 1.1: Moving Customer Update Records</strong></span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5;">1. Navigate to System Update Sets -&gt; Local Update Sets and click the New button on the Update Sets List View.</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">2. Name it something arbitrary.   This is for demonstration purposes only.   Do <strong>not</strong> make it your current update set.</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;"><img  __jive_id="45409" class="image-1 jive-image" src="cea3250edb5c130468c1fb651f9619eb.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 199px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">3. Make sure that your current update set is Default - Global.</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;"><img  __jive_id="45410" class="image-2 jive-image" src="12f14d86dbd097049c9ffb651f9619b3.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 52px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">4. Go make some modifications - easiest would be to modify a Form or List View.   Do two or three of these so as to show them up in the List View of Default. I modified the list views to add the Due Date to the Incident, Change, and Problem list views.</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">5. Go to the Global Default Update Set.</span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;"><img  __jive_id="45414" class="image-3 jive-image" src="9182ebf9db9093041dcaf3231f9619e8.iix" style="margin-right: auto; margin-left: auto; line-height: 26.6667px; width: 620px; height: 198px; display: block;"/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">6. Verify the changes are listed there.   I want to emphasize that you cannot move them through the Customer Updates List View in the Update Set.   You would have to do it one at a time from this location.</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;"><img  __jive_id="45415" class="jive-image image-4" src="6dfa810edb941b04ed6af3231f9619d8.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 390px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">7. Right-Click on the Customer Updates List View column header to bring up the context menu.   Click on Dictionary. </span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;"><img  __jive_id="45420" class="image-6 jive-image" src="229a6842dbd857041dcaf3231f961991.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 360px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">8. This will show the name of the table we want to go to: sys_update_xml</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;"><img  __jive_id="45421" class="image-7 jive-image" src="24f988cedb1017041dcaf3231f9619a1.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 270px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">9. From the Navigation Filter type: sys_update_xml.list to bring up the Customer Updates table.   This will display the Customer Updates List View. </span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;"><img  __jive_id="45422" class="jive-image image-8" src="b99ae3bddb981704ed6af3231f9619e1.iix" style="width: auto; height: auto; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;"><span style="font-size: 12pt;">10. If the Comments, Updated, and Updated By fields are missing then personalize the List View to add them.   </span><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Order the list by Updated date descending.</span></span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;"><img  __jive_id="45424" class="image-9 jive-image" src="0b9a2406db94dfc068c1fb651f9619eb.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 140px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">11. Note that your modifications for Default are listed.   Filter for the Default Update Set only; to get rid of the clutter.</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">12. Mass modify the comment field for those records you want to move to your new Update Set.   Something like: Update Set Demonstration</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;"><img  __jive_id="45425" class="jive-image image-10" src="29a6a0c2db1857041dcaf3231f961935.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 265px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">13. Now mass modify the Update Set field and change it to your new Update Set. </span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;"><img  __jive_id="45429" class="image-11 jive-image" src="2da50c0adb9c5704ed6af3231f9619c4.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 202px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">14. Go to Update Sets and edit up your new one.   Verify that the records were indeed moved to your new Update Set.</span></p><p></p><p><img  __jive_id="45430" class="image-12 jive-image" src="c2c4ab3ddbd093049c9ffb651f9619f9.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 363px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">There you have it!   That is the simple recovery.   Now for the one that proves to be a bit more troublesome!</span></p><p></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 14pt; line-height: 1.5;"><strong>Lab 1.2: Dealing With Duplicate Records</strong></span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 16px;">If a change already exists in the update set you are moving to; the one you are moving may be overridden.   Keep your wits about you and you won't have a problem, but it is good to review things after you do your move.   The best way to keep out of trouble is to use the Comment field method I mentioned here and in my previous </span><a title="" _jive_internal="true" href="/groups/servicenow-user-group-us-tx-north-texas/blog/2015/11/17/community-code-snippets-development-best-practices" style="font-size: 16px;">article</a><span style="font-size: 16px;">. </span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 16px;"><strong>Steps:</strong></span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">1. Make sure you are still on the Default Update Set.</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">2. Go make another change to the same List or Form View that you did earlier.   This makes a "duplicate" change to the same element that is already in your new update set, but was recorded in the Default Update Set.</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">3. Return to sys_update_xml.list.   Make sure you are ordered by Updated descending.</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">4. Note the values in the Type and Link columns for your most recent change.   Filter the list view on that Type and Link.</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">5. You should now see two records at the top.   One for your most recent change with the Update Set value of Default, and another older entry that appears to be the same change, but with the Update Set value of your new update set.</span></p><p></p><p><img  __jive_id="45432" class="image-13 jive-image" src="bc270946db541b04ed6af3231f9619e6.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 129px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">6. So now you can see the issue.   I usually set things up so I am looking at both Default and my new update set entries at the same time.   It helps.   Especially with the comment fields filled in.   So what to do?   As you know I really HATE deleting records, and prefer to hang onto them if at all possible.   You just never know if you are going to need that deleted information back, and once deleted it is gone forever.   So decision time.   I know that the latest modification is the one I want in my update set, but the now duplicated older record could cause a problem.   Since the sys_ids are different ServiceNow will let you move the record to your new update set, but that won't do!   </span><span style="font-size: 12pt;">You don't want both of them executing when you move your update set to another instance!   So a couple of possibilities:</span></p><p></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12pt;">1) Delete the older record, and move the new record to your update set. Not my favorite.</span></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12pt;">2) Export the older record to your hard-drive, delete it, and move the new record to your update set.   Well, it does preserve a backup, but still not optimal.</span></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12pt;">3) Create a new, temporary update set and merge all of your new changes with your new update set; letting ServiceNow sort out the change order.   This is my preference even though it can be a bit tedious if there are several of these.</span></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">So, let's go with number 3.</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The process:</span></p><p></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12pt;">1. Create a new temporary update set.</span></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12pt;"><img  __jive_id="45435" class="jive-image image-14" src="6c642842db90dfc068c1fb651f9619d7.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 336px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></span></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12pt;">2. From sys_update_xml move the duplicate records from Default to your temporary update set.</span></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12pt;"><img  __jive_id="45436" class="image-15 jive-image" src="794380cadbd8d3049c9ffb651f96193c.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 191px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></span></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12pt;">3. Go to your "temporary" update set, and merge it with the "new" update set to create yet another "even newer" update set!   Hey! You are the one that created the mess in the first place!   :-)</span></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12pt;"><img  __jive_id="45437" class="image-16 jive-image" src="f79aa40adb1857049c9ffb651f9619d1.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 317px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></span></p><p style="padding-left: 60px;"><span style="font-size: 12pt;">And then:</span></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12pt;"><img  __jive_id="45438" class="image-17 jive-image" src="d648044edb581344e9737a9e0f96192b.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 256px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></span></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"></p><p style="padding-left: 60px;"><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The merged updates set.   Note that the record without the comment is the newer one so replaced the original:</span></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"><img  __jive_id="45440" class="jive-image image-18" src="569ae739dbd09b048c8ef4621f96191a.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 382px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"></p><p style="padding-left: 60px;"><span style="font-size: 12pt;">NOTE: This is just a demonstration.   If this had really been a single record I probably would have gone with option 2.</span></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12pt;">4. Delete or Inactivate your the two update sets ("new" and "temporary").   You will now be moving forward with the "merged" update set.   Make it current, and you are good to go.</span></p><p style="padding-left: 30px;"></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5;"><strong>Recap</strong></span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">1. Remember to double-check your update set, and make sure it is not Default when making changes.</span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">2. That failing and you end up with records in the Default that you wanted in your other update set you now have a method for moving them.</span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">3. If you have it happen a second time, shame on you, and you find you have duplicates then you have a method for recovering from that mess as well, and hopefully you learned your lesson!</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Oh, and why do I know how to do all of this?   Because number 3 keeps happening to me!   :-)</span></p><p></p><p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-size: 12pt; font-family: inherit;">Steven Bell</span></p><p></p><p style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="color: #800080; font-weight: inherit; font-size: 12pt; font-family: inherit; font-style: inherit;"><strong>If you find this article helps you, don't forget to log in and "like" it!</strong></span></p><p></p><p style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="color: #800080; font-weight: inherit; font-size: 12pt; font-family: inherit; font-style: inherit;"><strong>I would also like to encourage you to become a member of our blog!</strong></span></p>
