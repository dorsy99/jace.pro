<p>After many strategic months of hard work, our team rolled out our new <a title="ki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Migrating_to_Knowledge_Management_v3#gsc.tab=0" href="http://wiki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Migrating_to_Knowledge_Management_v3#gsc.tab=0">Knowledge Management v3 module</a> in HI on Friday, May 1st! The goal with v3 was to simplify the module, make it easier to use and understand, and at the same time, add features that make organizing and developing Knowledge a cinch. The new module is available starting with the Fuji release.</p><p></p><p>What's different? Besides the updated user interface, which you may have become acquainted with in Fuji, here are some of the most important changes you'll notice in HI.</p><ul><li>Module looks a bit different</li><li>Multiple knowledge bases.</li><li>Separate, customizable workflows available for each knowledge base.</li><li>Category structure with any number of levels. (Each knowledge base has a unique category taxonomy.)</li><li>Permissions defined per knowledge base using user criteria.</li></ul><p><span style="color: #333333; font-family: Omnes-pro, Arial, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 14px;"><br/></span></p><p>The knowledge modules also changed slightly. The new module list looks like this:</p><p style="margin-top: 0.4em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; font-size: 14px; color: #333333; font-family: Omnes-pro, Arial, Verdana, sans-serif;"><img  __jive_id="19566" alt="Knowledge_mgmt_menus_modules.jpg" class="image-0 jive-image jiveImage" height="373" src="ef898c06db509704ed6af3231f9619bc.iix" style="height: 373.250965250965px; width: 152px; float: none;" width="152"/></p><p></p><p></p><h3>Multiple knowledge bases and multiple search options, depending on your role and access</h3><p><span style="color: #575757;">Knowledge v3 takes advantage of the new features in Fuji that allow for multiple knowledge bases and more efficient organization. ServiceNow knowledge bases appear on the Knowledge homepage, and the articles are organized by their respective knowledge base and category. Articles are also prioritized according to feature content and popular articles and <a _jive_internal="true" href="/groups/servicenow-user-group-sig-knowledge-management/blog/2015/03/04/bumping-an-article-to-the-top-of-a-search-result-set"><span style="color: #575757;">you can pin articles</span></a>. </span><span style="color: #575757; font-size: 10pt; font-family: Omnes-pro, Arial, Verdana, sans-serif;">Here's what the new knowledge home screen looks like with multiple knowledge bases listed.</span></p><p></p><p><img   alt="Knowledge-Homepage.png" class="image-0 jive-image" src="d25c6779db9893049c9ffb651f961955.iix" style="height: auto; float: none;"/></p><p></p><p></p><h3>Enhanced searching</h3><p>From the Knowledge homepage, you can select a knowledge base, and then search for an article within that KB. Keep in mind that your search will only pull up articles that you have access to, based on your role. Here's what your search results might look like.</p><p></p><p><img   alt="Knowledge-Homepage-Category.png" class="image-1 jive-image" src="7ac90546db941b04ed6af3231f96193d.iix" style="height: 426px; width: 620px; float: none;"/></p><p></p><p></p><p style="margin-top: 0.4em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; font-size: 14px; color: #333333; font-family: Omnes-pro, Arial, Verdana, sans-serif;">You can also search for articles using the search bar at the top of the page and then sort results by relevancy, most recent update, or number of views. You can also filter results by selecting specific knowledge bases to search, selecting one or more categories, and by selecting one or more authors.</p><p style="margin-top: 0.4em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; font-size: 14px; color: #333333; font-family: Omnes-pro, Arial, Verdana, sans-serif;">You can sort results by relevancy, most recent update, or number of views. Your filtered search results might look something like this:</p><p style="margin-top: 0.4em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; font-size: 14px; color: #333333; font-family: Omnes-pro, Arial, Verdana, sans-serif;"><img   alt="Km_search_filter.png" class="image-0 jive-image" src="998aac0edbd4d704ed6af3231f961991.iix" style="height: 245px; width: 620px; float: none;"/></p><p></p><p></p><h3>Feedback</h3><p style="margin-top: 0.4em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; font-size: 14px; color: #333333; font-family: Omnes-pro, Arial, Verdana, sans-serif;">As in legacy versions of Knowledge, you can view and contribute feedback on articles, but with v3, we hope you find it a little more intuitive to use.
