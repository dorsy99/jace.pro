<p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">Are legal requests similar to IT requests? HR requests? Facilities requests? You bet. <strong>Everything</strong> as a Service. </span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">The Fuji release of ServiceNow includes a useful <a href="http://wiki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Legal_Service_Management"><span style="color: #0000ff; text-decoration: underline;">Legal Service Management</span></a> application that enables you to create trackable legal requests. Your legal department can organize, manage, and view the work they routinely do such as contracts, legal research, and policy questions. This automation can give the legal eagles more time to focus on strategic and thorny problems that can't be automated as easily.</span></p><p></p><h2>Submitting a legal request:</h2><p></p><p>The legal process starts when users <a href="http://wiki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Managing_Legal_Requests#Creating_Legal_Requests"><span style="color: #0000ff; text-decoration: underline;">submit a request</span></a> through the Service Catalog.</p><ol><li>Navigate to<strong> Self-Service &gt; Legal Catalog</strong>.<br/><img   alt="legal1.jpg" class="image-0 jive-image" src="4a116f7ddb9c9fc0b322f4621f961901.iix" style="height: 279px; width: 620px;"/></li><li>Click an option such as <strong>Contract</strong> or <strong>Research</strong>. A menu of items appears.</li><li>Click an item. For example, if you click <strong>Contract</strong>, you'll see items such as <strong>New NDA Creation Request</strong>, <strong>Vendor RFP Review</strong>, and<strong> External Contract Review</strong>:<br/><img   alt="legal2.jpg" class="image-1 jive-image" src="97219182dbd4130468c1fb651f96195c.iix" style="height: auto;"/></li><li>Fill in as many fields as possible. It is especially important to provide a detailed description of the request and set the priority appropriately.</li><li>Click <strong>Submit</strong>.</li></ol><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"><br/></span></p><h2>Managing legal requests:</h2><p></p><p>The base system legal work <a href="http://wiki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Managing_Legal_Requests"><span style="color: #0000ff; text-decoration: underline;">follows a life cycle</span></a>:</p><ul><li>After a request is submitted, a legal staff member reviews the request, adds any additional work information, and marks the request as ready for approval</li><li>A legal approver reviews the request</li><li>If the request is approved, it is assigned to a group or an individual</li><li>If the request is complex, requires people with different skills, or has steps that must be scheduled sequentially, you can create multiple tasks and assign them appropriately</li><li>Work takes place - communication with the submitter can be done through the Additional Comments field and collaboration with others working on the request can be done in the Work Notes field</li><li>The legal tasks and request are closed complete, closed incomplete, or cancelled</li></ul><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"><br/></span></p><h2>Tracking legal requests:</h2><p></p><p>Similar to other ServiceNow applications, there is an up to date dashboard that displays information including requests by state, requests by month, and performance visually.</p><p><img   alt="legal3.jpg" class="image-0 jive-image" src="4e6be0c6db18d704ed6af3231f96191d.iix" style="height: 427px; width: 620px;"/></p><p>Several useful legal reports are also available:</p><p><img   alt="legal4.jpg" class="image-1 jive-image" src="6a505846db505304b322f4621f961986.iix" style="height: auto;"/></p><p></p><p></p><h2>Configuring the Legal Service Management application:</h2><p></p><p>I have outlined the base system (out of box) process with the Legal Service Management application, but in true ServiceNow fashion, the application can be <a href="http://wiki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Legal_Configuration"><span style="color: #0000ff; text-decoration: underline;">customized to suit your needs</span></a>. You may want to start out using a simple request-driven process with minimal layers of approval and then make changes over time to add greater control and more sophisticated task assignment. For example:</p><p></p><ul><li>Switch from the default request-driven process for handling tasks to a task-driven process where multiple tasks must be done before requests are complete</li><li>Make approvals required</li><li>Automatically assign requests</li><li>Automatically assign tasks</li><li>Fine tune automatic assignment of tasks based on agent location and skills</li></ul><p></p><p>For more information about Legal Service Management and the greater Service Management world, see the following:</p><p></p><p><a __default_attr="4094" __jive_macro_name="blogpost" class="jive_macro jive_macro_blogpost" data-orig-content="Service Management for the Enterprise " href="/community?id=community_blog&sys_id=ea1d62e5dbd0dbc01dcaf3231f961939" modifiedtitle="true" title="Service Management for the Enterprise ">Service Management for the Enterprise </a>(informative videos)</p><p><a __default_attr="4144" __jive_macro_name="blogpost" class="jive_macro jive_macro_blogpost" data-orig-content="Now on Now: Legal Management as a Service" href="/community?id=community_blog&sys_id=e52d26e5dbd0dbc01dcaf3231f9619d5" modifiedtitle="true" title="Now on Now: Legal Management as a Service">Now on Now: Legal Management as a Service</a> (great K15 legal session review by <a __default_attr="24805" __jive_macro_name="user" class="jive_macro_user jive_macro" data-orig-content="Morgan Hunter " href="/community?id=community_user_profile&user=de11d625db981fc09c9ffb651f961936" modifiedtitle="true" title="Morgan Hunter ">Morgan Hunter </a>- thanks, Morgan!) </p><p><a href="http://wiki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Service_Management#gsc.tab=0" title="http://wiki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Service_Management#gsc.tab=0">Service Management </a>(overview of Service Management in the ServiceNow product documentation</p>
