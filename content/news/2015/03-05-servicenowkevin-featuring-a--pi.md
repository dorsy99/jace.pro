<p>The ability to <a title="ki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Managing_Knowledge#Pinning_Articles" href="http://wiki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Managing_Knowledge#Pinning_Articles">pin an article to the top of a search result</a> set based on the users search has been a highly requested functionality. As of today, with the launch of Fuji, we actually have that capability. You're able to pin articles to the top of search result sets based on keywords that relate to that specific article.</p><p></p><p>As an example, if our customers wanted to search on "email notifications" and we happen to have an article that explains everything there is to know about email notifications, we would want to pin it at the top of the result set. W<span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">e could use "email," "emailing," and "notifications" as our keywords to bring that article up. </span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"><em>**Note that as of right now you can only use single words, not phrases. I've asked development to include support for search phrases instead of search terms. No word on when that might happen just yet.</em></span></p><p></p><p>As an added bonus, you can also <a title="ki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Configuring_Knowledge_Properties#Knowledge_Homepage_Properties" href="http://wiki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Configuring_Knowledge_Properties#Knowledge_Homepage_Properties">pin articles to home pages </a><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">of the knowledge base. That is, the main KB home page and individual home pages (because we support multiple knowledge bases now) can have featured pinned content.</span></p><p></p><p><a href="http://wiki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Using_Knowledge#Viewing_Knowledge"><img   alt="KBhomepage.png" class="image-0 jive-image" src="5e1c7ff9dbd45704ed6af3231f9619c5.iix" style="height: 272px; width: 620px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></a></p><p></p><p>We'll be rolling out the <a title="ki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Knowledge_Management" href="http://wiki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Knowledge_Management">Fuji version of Knowledge Management </a>in a few weeks. As soon as we do, I'll be working with the team to get articles pinned based on common search terms. Specifically, we'll be pinning things like our <a title="i.service-now.com/kb_view.do?sysparm_article=KB0540193" href="https://hi.service-now.com/kb_view.do?sysparm_article=KB0540193">resource pages</a>.</p><p></p><p><strong>Additional Info</strong></p><p></p><p><a title="Tom Alday" __default_attr="11609" __jive_macro_name="user" class="jive_macro_user jive_macro" data-orig-content="Tom Alday" href="/community?id=community_user_profile&user=e981dea9db981fc09c9ffb651f9619c3">Tom Alday</a> you weren't the only person with a question, so I thought I'd see if I could show the steps.</p><p></p><ol><li>Click Knowledge Bases.</li><li>Choose the knowledge base to which you want to add the pinned article. In my example, I'll be using Knowledge.</li><li>Click the Featured Content tab.</li><li>Click New.<br/><img   alt="featured content.png" class="image-0 jive-image" src="4674e142db18d7041dcaf3231f9619c3.iix" style="height: 166px; width: 620px;"/></li><li>Add the article ID.<br/><img   alt="ArticleID.png" class="image-1 jive-image" src="186c3042db5097049c9ffb651f961931.iix" style="height: auto;"/></li><li>Click the lock icon next to Keywords.</li><li>If you've added keywords before, start typing in keywords. If you haven't, click the magnifying glass.</li><li>Click New.</li><li>Type in the keyword and click Submit.<br/><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"><img   alt="Keyword.png" class="jive-image image-3" src="2bea94cadbd45344e9737a9e0f961909.iix" style="height: 93px; width: 620px;"/><br/>The keyword is added to the keywords list for that article.<br/></span></li><li><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">Click Submit.</span></li><li><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">Return to the KB home page. In my example, I went to the Knowledge home page.</span></li><li><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">Perform a search. Note the result has a blue tinted background.<br/><img   alt="pinnedresult.png" class="jive-image image-4" src="564ab3fddb14d3049c9ffb651f961911.iix" style="height: 144px; width: 620px;"/></span></li></ol><p>If you want to edit the keywords for an article that already has keywords:</p><ol><li>From the Featured Content tab, click the keywords associated to that article.</li><li>Modify the keywords field as necessary.</li><li>Click Update.</li></ol><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"><br/></span></p>
