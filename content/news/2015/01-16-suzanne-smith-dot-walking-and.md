<p>Dot-walking got its descriptive name because, when dot-walking, you reference a field by building a chain of field names separated by dots. For example, <em>incident.change_request.state</em> references the state of the change request associated with an incident. Dot-walking is commonly used when personalizing forms, so I'll discuss the two together in this post.</p><p></p><p style="text-align: center;"><em>Dot-walking is one of my favorite ServiceNow terms</em></p><p><img   alt="dot_walk2.jpg" class="image-0 jive-image" height="194" src="3f16edc2db5cd304b322f4621f96197f.iix" style="height: 193.964516129032px; width: 393px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="393"/></p><p></p><p></p><p>If you are using ServiceNow, chances are you have right-clicked a form header and selected <strong>Personalize &gt; Form Layout </strong>(beginning with the Fuji release, you'll select <strong>Configure &gt; Form Layout</strong>)<strong>.</strong> This magical feature enables administrators and users with the personalize_form role to customize a form. The base system Incident form not working for you? Configure it. Use the slushbucket to move fields on and off the form and arrange them. For example, select the <strong>Business Duration</strong> field in the <strong>Available</strong> list and click the <strong>Add</strong> button to move the field to the <strong>Selected</strong> list.</p><p></p><p></p><p><img   alt="dot_walk3.jpg" class="image-0 jive-image" src="dad1498adb1897041dcaf3231f9619f7.iix" style="height: auto; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></p><p>In the slushbucket, notice that some of the entires in the <strong>Available</strong> list are displayed in green and have a plus <span __jive_emoticon_name="plus" __jive_macro_name="emoticon" class="jive_macro jive_emote" src="/6.0.3.0/images/emoticons/plus.png"></span> sign next to them. These entries represent related tables. Click the plus sign to dot-walk and access all the fields on the related table. For example, click <strong>Change Request [+]</strong> and then click the green plus sign above <strong>Add</strong>.</p><p></p><p></p><p><img   alt="dot_walk4.jpg" class="jive-image image-2" src="98c3200edbd057049c9ffb651f9619d7.iix" style="height: auto; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></p><p><br/>The <strong>Available</strong> list now shows the Change Request fields:</p><p></p><p></p><p style="margin-top: 0.4em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; font-size: 14px; color: #333333; font-family: Omnes-pro, Arial, Verdana, sans-serif;"><img   alt="dot_walk5.jpg" class="jive-image image-3" src="85c2c082dbd85fc068c1fb651f961922.iix" style="height: auto; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></p><p style="margin-top: 0.4em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; font-size: 14px; color: #333333; font-family: Omnes-pro, Arial, Verdana, sans-serif;"><br/>Select any field in the <strong>Available</strong> list and click <strong>Add</strong> to add the field to the <strong>Incident</strong> form. This enables you to, for example, more closely track change requests created from incidents. <span style="color: #333333; font-family: Omnes-pro, Arial, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 14px;">Note that the next time you open the slushbucket, the Change Request fields are not expanded and shown in the </span><span style="color: #333333; font-family: Omnes-pro, Arial, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 14px;"><strong>Available</strong></span><span style="color: #333333; font-family: Omnes-pro, Arial, Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 14px;"> list - to view them again you need to click the plus sign again. </span>To list the fields on the Incident form again, click <strong>Incident fields</strong> at the top of the <strong>Available</strong> list.</p><p style="margin-top: 0.4em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; font-size: 14px; color: #333333; font-family: Omnes-pro, Arial, Verdana, sans-serif; text-align: center;"><img   alt="dot_walk7.jpg" class="jive-image image-2" src="729ff086db5013043eb27a9e0f961925.iix" style="height: auto;"/></p><p style="margin-top: 0.4em; margin-bottom: 0.5em; font-size: 14px; color: #333333; font-family: Omnes-pro, Arial, Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"><br/></span></p><h2>Tips for dot-walking</h2><p></p><p>The dot-walking possibilities are endless when you are personalizing forms. Here are some tips.</p><ul><li>Users frequently ask about the gear feature as compared to the Personalize List feature. They are different. When a user clicks the gear icon, the slushbucket only lists fields on the current table. With the Personalize List feature, the slushbucket lists related tables and allows you to dot-walk to them. Also, if you make changes using the gear, the changes are visible only to you, but if you personalize a form, the changes are seen by all users with access to the form and <a href="http://wiki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=View_Management"><span style="color: #0000ff; text-decoration: underline;">view</span></a>.<br/><img   alt="dot_walk6.jpg" class="image-0 jive-image" src="b404a18edbd4d7041dcaf3231f9619da.iix" style="height: 102px; width: 620px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></li><li>In order to dot-walk, you must have read access to the target table and field.
