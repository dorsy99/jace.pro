<p>I had a customer want to set up a notification that users could subscribe to. They wanted the notifications to be email and SMS. I could not find any real good step by step explanation on how to do this. That is why I am posting this blog. <br /><br />In this example we will set up a notification that sends email or SMS to users when the Impact of an incident is set to 1. There are two ways to trigger notifications. First, <b>you can trigger it from an insert or update of a record in a table.</b> Second, <b>you can trigger it from an event with a business rule.</b> Below are examples of each way:<br /><br /><ol><li><b>Create Notification to be triggered from an insert or update of a record in a table</b></li><li style="list-style: none"><br /> <ol><li>Make sure outbound email is set up and working. See WIKI http://wiki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Configuring_Email for this.</li><li>Create New Notification</li><li style="list-style: none"><br /> <ol><li>System Policy | Notification</li><li>Click New</li><li>Click Advanced View</li><li>Name it "Incident Impact Changes to 1"</li><li>Make sure the table is set to incident</li><li>Make sure the Type is set to "EMAIL"</li><li style="list-style: none"><br /> <img src="http://www.hobbycache.com/images/servicenow/img1.jpg" width="600" height="112" /><br /></li><li>On the "When to send" tab, set the "Send when" to Record inserted or updated</li><li>Check the inserted and updated checkboxes</li><li>Conditions: Impact "is 1" or Impact is "less than or is 2"</li><li style="list-style: none"><br /> <img src="http://www.hobbycache.com/images/servicenow/img2.jpg" width="600" height="280" /><br /></li><li>On the "Who will receive" tab, set Subscribeable to on</li><li style="list-style: none"><br /> <img src="http://www.hobbycache.com/images/servicenow/img3.jpg" width="600" height="237" /><br /></li><li>On the "What it will contain" tab, fill in Subject and Message with variables and SMS alternate also</li><li style="list-style: none"><br /> <img src="http://www.hobbycache.com/images/servicenow/img4.jpg" width="600" height="296" /><br /></li></ol><br /></li><li>Click on "My Profile" under Self Service</li><li>Click on "Notification Preferences" Related Link</li><li>By default you have "Primary Email" set up. You can click "New Device" to add your phone</li><li>Click the green + button to subscribe to your notification and select "Incident Impact Changes to 1", pick your device and submit it.</li><li>Create or update an incident with the impact 1</li><li>Look at email logs to see emails sent under System Logs | Emails. The status of "send-ready" means it is going to send and "sent" status means it went out.</li></ol><br /><br /><br /><br /><br /></li><li><b>Create Notification to be triggered from an event with a business rule</b></li><li style="list-style: none"><br /> <ol><li>Make sure outbound email is set up and working. See WIKI http://wiki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Configuring_Email for this.</li><li>Create and register a new Event</li><li style="list-style: none"><br /> <ol><li>System Policy | Events | Registry</li><li>Name it something like "incident.impact.1"</li><li>Make sure the table is set to incident</li><li>Fill in the rest of the fields as desired</li><li style="list-style: none"><br /> <img src="http://www.hobbycache.com/images/servicenow/img5.jpg" width="600" height="116" /><br /></li></ol><br /></li><li>Create a business rule similar to "Incident events" with the following script</li><li style="list-style: none"><br /> <pre __default_attr="plain" __jive_macro_name="code" class="jive_text_macro jive_macro_code"><br /><br />          if (current.impact.changes() &amp;amp;&amp;amp; current.impact == 1) {<br />               gs.eventQueue("incident.impact.1", current, current.impact, previous.impact);<br />          }<br />         </pre><br /> <img src="http://www.hobbycache.com/images/servicenow/img6.jpg" width="600" height="134" /><br /></li><li>Create New Notification</li><li style="list-style: none"><br /> <ol><li>System Policy | Notification</li><li>Click New</li><li>Click Advanced View</li><li>Name it "Incident Impact Changes to 1"</li><li>Make sure the table is set to incident</li><li>Make sure the Type is set to "EMAIL"</li><li style="list-style: none"><br /> <img src="http://www.hobbycache.com/images/servicenow/img1.jpg" width="600" height="112" /><br /></li><li>On the "When to send" tab, set the "Send when" to Event is fired</li><li>Event name: choose your event that you created in step 2.</li><li style="list-style: none"><br /> <img src="http://www.hobbycache.com/images/servicenow/img7.jpg" width="600" height="224" /><br /></li><li>On the "Who will receive" tab, set Subscribeable to on</li><li style="list-style: none"><br /> <img src="http://www.hobbycache.com/images/servicenow/img8.jpg" width="600" height="192" /><br /></li><li>On the "What it will contain" tab, fill in Subject and Message with variables and SMS alternate also</li><li style="list-style: none"><br /> <img src="http://www.hobbycache.com/images/servicenow/img4.jpg" width="600" height="296" /><br /></li></ol><br /></li><li>Click on "My Profile" under Self Service</li><li>Click on "Notification Preferences" Related Link</li><li>By default you have "Primary Email" set up. You can click "New Device" to add your phone</li><li>Click the green + button to subscribe to your notification and select "Incident Impact Changes to 1", pick your device and submit it.</li><li>Create or update an incident with the impact 1</li><li>Look at email logs to see emails sent under System Logs | Emails. The status of "send-ready" means it is going to send and "sent" status means it went out.</li></ol><br /></li></ol></p>
