<p class="BodyA" style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit; font-weight: inherit;"><em>Here is a blog I wrote years ago on, well, blogging. I hope it helps some of you translate your thoughts and knowledge into compact, clear and useful content!</em></span></p><p class="BodyA" style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-family: 'Lucida Grande';"><br/></span></p><p class="BodyA" style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-family: 'Lucida Grande';">&gt;&gt;</span></p><p class="BodyA" style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-family: 'Lucida Grande';">Writing isn't so hard when nobody's going to read it. Like me, you might be able to fill three journal pages in ten minutes of scribbling. But if I'm on a deadline, I run into two opposing problems:</span></p><p class="BodyA" style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><ol style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><li><span style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-family: 'Lucida Grande'; text-decoration: underline;">Writer's block</span><span style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-family: 'Lucida Grande';">. A form of anxiety whereby nothing seems perfect enough to make it onto the page. Everything in my head sounds rough-drafty and not at all ready for critical eyes. </span></li><li><span style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-family: 'Lucida Grande'; text-decoration: underline;">Inability to self-edit</span><span style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-family: 'Lucida Grande';">. Deleting my own words means detaching my emotions from the time and energy I just spent creating them. Plus, I like what I wrote and the way it's phrased. I just can't bring myself to cut anything.</span></li></ol><p class="BodyA" style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p class="BodyA" style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-family: 'Lucida Grande';">These are self-inflicted problems, of course — ones I'm sure I share with many. Recently, however, I took some time to observe my own writing process over 4 drafts of one piece. As a result, I came up with some tips to ensure quick, smooth, succinct writing, every time.</span></p><p class="BodyA" style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p class="BodyA" style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-family: 'Lucida Grande';">To become a ruthless self-editor and much better writer, you simply need to:</span></p><p class="BodyA" style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p class="BodyA" style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit; font-weight: inherit;"><strong>Start with research.</strong></span></p><p class="BodyA" style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-family: 'Lucida Grande';">Read your assignment sheet (if you have one), do some third-party reading, and become well-versed on the subject. Take your time with this, and trust that your brain absorbs information whether or not you're conscious of it.</span></p><p class="BodyA" style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p class="BodyA" style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit; font-weight: inherit;"><strong>Then, just write.</strong></span></p><p class="BodyA" style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-family: 'Lucida Grande';">As they say, "don't get it right, get it written". Record your thoughts on what you researched in any order. What would your audience want to know? What kinds of problems do they face? What do they care most about?</span></p><p class="BodyA" style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p class="BodyA" style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-family: 'Lucida Grande';">(P.S. I started this very blog post in the exact same way. I wrote in a stream-of-conscious, head-clearing manner for a whole hour. Then I took a break and came back to it with new ideas and a fresh perspective.)</span></p><p class="BodyA" style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p class="BodyA" style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit; font-weight: inherit;"><strong>Do take a break.</strong></span></p><p class="BodyA" style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-family: 'Lucida Grande';">When you return, look for some trends in your notes. Do any themes build on their own? Is there a logical outline or structure to the piece? Start to cut and paste and move things around. Refine your wording, grammar, and vocabulary as needed.</span></p><p class="BodyA" style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p class="BodyA" style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit; font-weight: inherit;"><strong>Think chronologically.</strong></span></p><p class="BodyA" style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-family: 'Lucida Grande';">If someone was to actually take your advice, what would they do first? Write your blogs like a lively, entertaining instruction manual. This will also help you understand the topic better.</span></p><p class="BodyA" style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p class="BodyA" style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit; font-weight: inherit;"><strong>Think, "who cares?"</strong></span></p><p class="BodyA" style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-family: 'Lucida Grande';">Ask yourself why you included each sentence. Cut the fluff: does every paragraph say something new or useful? You probably said the same thing twice somewhere. Admit it, then cut it. It's freeing.</span></p><p class="BodyA" style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p class="BodyA" style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit; font-weight: inherit;"><strong>Give it shape.</strong></span></p><p class="BodyA" style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-family: 'Lucida Grande';">Define paragraphs, lists, and create more white space. Basically, you want to take a big idea and turn it into bite-sized chunks. If that means you end up turning one blog into two, great! More content for you and your readers.</span></p><p class="BodyA" style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p class="BodyA" style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit; font-weight: inherit;"><strong>Be ruthless.</strong></span></p><p class="BodyA" style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-family: 'Lucida Grande';">Ask yourself, if I had to cut the word count in HALF, what would stay and what would go? This will cause you to write the highest quality, most information-dense sentences and paragraphs you can. You don't actually have to delete half of your content, but pretend like you do.</span></p><p class="BodyA" style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p class="BodyA" style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit; font-weight: inherit;"><strong>Practice makes...</strong></span></p><p style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-family: 'Lucida Grande';">Better. You won't get it right on the first try. Don't fool yourself into thinking you will, either. No writer is perfect, not even Thoreau. (Ok maybe, but you get the idea.)</span></p><p class="BodyA" style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p class="NormalWeb1" style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit; font-size: 12pt; font-family: 'Lucida Grande'; color: #141414;">Now go on, write it out like nobody's watching.</span></p>
