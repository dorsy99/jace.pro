<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;"><strong>[4/30 — 18:30pm] Meet Rich, who hardly knew ServiceNow but created anyway</strong></span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">One of the most remarkable contestants in this year&#39;s CreateNow Hackathon is a young man called Rich Acosta, who is a UNIX Engineer at a Financial Services firm out of New Jersey, NY. Rumor has it Rich joined the Hackathon with <em>less than a day</em> of ServiceNow experience under his belt. Nevertheless, and on his own, he produced an app to measure audience sentiment in Twitter. Mike Lusk (Director of Americas Marketing Programs) and I met up with him to get some detail. </span></p>
<p> </p>
<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">- So Rich, what&#39;s your story, buddy?   </span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">&#34;I have some experience working with Twitter and I have always been into data analytics. So I created a <a title="w.hootsuite.com/" href="http://www.hootsuite.com/" rel="nofollow">Hootsuite</a>-like interface in ServiceNow, connected to Twitter, and integrated with a ServiceNow approval workflow. This way, employees create tweets, but before they&#39;re pushed to Twitter, the manager has to approve.&#34;</span></p>
<p> </p>
<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">- OK, but wouldn&#39;t that create a huge workload for the managers once all employees start shooting off tweets?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">&#34;Yeah you are right, we don&#39;t want the management to be bottlenecks. So next, I pulled a sentiment analysis algorithm off of the internet. Similar principle to the kind of thing they used to get IBM&#39;s Watson to understand natural language. It produces a smiley (positive sentiment), a very unhappy smiley (negative sentiment) or a neutral smiley (neutral sentiment). Smileys go straight to Twitter, Neutrals go to management and unhappy faces get rejected straight away.&#34;</span></p>
<p> </p>
<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">- …and you&#39;re sure you have less than a day of experience in ServiceNow..?     </span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">I guess so. I had two hours of labs the day before and then some presentations on Tuesday. But I already mentioned I have some good experience with the Twitter API. So…</span></p>
<p> </p>
<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">- Yeah you did mention that. OK, and where are you going to take the ServiceNow platform at work? What are you guys looking into?</span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">We want to introduce ServiceNow Service Catalogues and use ServiceNow as our Service Automation platform. Personally, I am also interested to learn more about your CMDB capabilities. I work with and maintain whole range of legacy homegrown systems, and I&#39;m looking to retire some and replace others with ServiceNow. Get everything recorded and maintained. But first I&#39;ll finish my masters degree in Computing &amp; Business at NJIT in May. Is it already May?</span></p>
<p> </p>
<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">- No Rich. It&#39;s only April 30<sup>th</sup>. You have some more time to discover ServiceNow. We sure hope to see you next year!</span></p>

<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;"><strong>[4/30 — 17:45pm] Password Vault for shared corporate accounts</strong></span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">&#34;When it comes to password management and credentials, no one wants shared accounts. But you can&#39;t always avoid it. Think about an account for some external service, a backup account or even just a corporate Youtube account. Therefore it is better to control it and have visibility.&#34; I am talking <a title="" href="community?id&#61;community_user_profile&amp;user&#61;caf252e1db1c1fc09c9ffb651f961916" rel="nofollow">Jason Norred</a>, Director of Service Management at <a title="w.pcm.ca/" href="http://www.pcm.ca/" rel="nofollow">PCM</a>, a Managed Service Provider. </span></p>
<p> </p>
<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">Jason and his team of ServiceNow Developers — <a title="" href="community?id&#61;community_user_profile&amp;user&#61;9660da69db581fc09c9ffb651f9619a4" rel="nofollow">James Carroll</a> and <a title="" href="community?id&#61;community_user_profile&amp;user&#61;6cafce2ddb181fc09c9ffb651f9619b6" rel="nofollow">George Floratos</a> — were excited to enter the Hackathon. &#34;But we wanted to put it to good use. Last year, we had some fun, created a game. This year, we wanted to focus on an app that we can bring back to our 100&#43; engineers on Monday.&#34;</span></p>
<p> </p>
<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">&#34;If not controlled,&#34; he continued, &#34;these passwords reside with a couple of guys within the IT department. Nobody knows when a given password was last changed. Nobody wants to go ahead and change it, because it is a pain to communicate it out to the right co-workers. So nothing changes, people leave, passwords get lost. It&#39;s all quite risky. We remotely manage services for our clients and we see this happen all the time — so we are genuinely looking for a solution.&#34;</span></p>
<p> </p>
<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">&#34;We created a password vault to store credentials in. The app generates a secured password and we use orchestration to make the change a reality. The credentials are encrypted and we use Access Control List (ACL) to make sure you see only the credentials that you are supposed to see in your role. On top, you take a deliberate action to access it — therefore that activity is recorded. If a system is compromised in any way, we can see who last retrieved the password for it. It creates visibility and control around a topic that is quite often neglected.&#34;</span></p>

<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;"><strong>[4/30 - 17:05pm] San Francisco needs CableCarNow!</strong></span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">The app of Team CableCarNow was born out of frustration. The world famous cable cars of San Francisco are always full and the queues at the stops are way too long. &#34;So the city needs a solution and it needs it fast,&#34; said Melhem El-Achkar, who is a managing partner at <a title="w.ncc-solutions.com/" href="http://www.ncc-solutions.com/" rel="nofollow">ncc solutions</a> out of Munich, Germany. &#34;This is our second Hackathon, with some slight changes to the team. Last year, we created CasinoNow, a blackjack app that got us great acclaim. This year, we&#39;re not playing games; we address a real issue in this city.&#34;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;"> </span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;"><img class="image-0 jive-image" style="height: 286px; width: 381.3333333333333px; float: right;" src="34146b31dbdc9f04e9737a9e0f961975.iix" alt="Line.jpeg" width="381" height="286" /></span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">Our app has two parts: one part is consumer-facing, one is the back-office where the cable cars are managed. Through an API with Google Maps, consumers can see where the cable cars are. Using sensors, we&#39;d push back information on the length of the queue and the capacity of the cars. That way, consumers know when they can expect t find an available spot. They can then purchase tickets online using the mobile app. </span></p>
<p> </p>
<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">Because all variables — cars, routes, stations — are Configuration Items, we can apply the full service management capabilities to this story. We incident and asset management to keep track of the status of our cable cars, we use financial management to track tickets sold. Incident and problem management for cars that break down. We have it all covered. The city needs this!&#34;</span></p>
<p> </p>
<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">CableCarNow consists of <a title="" href="community?id&#61;community_user_profile&amp;user&#61;e7d05e21db981fc09c9ffb651f96192a" rel="nofollow">Melhem El-Achkar</a>, <a title="" href="community?id&#61;community_user_profile&amp;user&#61;935f4629db181fc09c9ffb651f961979" rel="nofollow">Konstantin Bazanov</a> and <a title="" href="community?id&#61;community_user_profile&amp;user&#61;12e09661db981fc09c9ffb651f9619d9" rel="nofollow">Thomas Fregin</a> of ncc solutions, <a title="" href="community?id&#61;community_user_profile&amp;user&#61;4ff11aa1dbd81fc09c9ffb651f96190a" rel="nofollow">Chris Bailey</a> of Pro Health Care and <a title="" href="community?id&#61;community_user_profile&amp;user&#61;52629a69dbd81fc09c9ffb651f961980" rel="nofollow">Maxime Richard</a> of Imakumo.   </span></p>

<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;"><strong>[4/30 - 14.45pm] Meet MudMonsters, a text adventure game</strong></span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt; line-height: 1.5em;">Scott Brian, one of last year&#39;s Leftover&#39;s</span><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt; line-height: 1.5em;">, came alone and found his team members </span><a style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt; line-height: 1.5em;" title="" href="community?id&#61;community_user_profile&amp;user&#61;31705aa9db581fc09c9ffb651f961944" rel="nofollow">But ch Istook</a><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt; line-height: 1.5em;"> of Intel Corp, and </span><a style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt; line-height: 1.5em;" title="" href="community?id&#61;community_user_profile&amp;user&#61;bc7fca69db181fc09c9ffb651f9619cf" rel="nofollow">Hunter Wolf</a><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt; line-height: 1.5em;"> and </span><a style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt; line-height: 1.5em;" title="" href="community?id&#61;community_user_profile&amp;user&#61;88005ae1db581fc09c9ffb651f9619e8" rel="nofollow">Erik Brostrom</a><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt; line-height: 1.5em;"> of CH Robinson. Together, they created a mult-user, text-based action adventure. Using text commands, the players can fight, do and take damage, solve puzzles, and inspect items. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt; line-height: 1.5em;"> </span></p>
<p><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt; line-height: 1.5em;">&#34;We have 35 locations, 12 monsters, many items in there,&#34; Scott explained. &#34;We use business rules to govern the behavior of all elements. The business rules trigger events based off of the text body in the commands. You win when you reach the final destination. If you loose, you go back to square one and you loose all items.&#34; </span></p>
