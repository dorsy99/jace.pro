<p>Controlling <strong>New</strong> and <strong>Edit</strong> buttons on related lists, particularly setting where they appear and who sees them, can be a pesky problem. <a title="" _jive_internal="true" class="jiveTT-hover-user jive-link-profile-small" data-containerid="-1" data-containertype="-1" data-objectid="2952" data-objecttype="3" href="/community?id=community_user_profile&user=95c096eddb581fc09c9ffb651f9619f2">Matthew Watkins</a> in our Customer Support group has written some articles on this subject and I'd like to summarize his fine work.</p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p><span style="color: #485563; font-family: arial; font-size: 13px; text-align: -webkit-auto;">Editing the <strong>New</strong> and <strong>Edit</strong> buttons requires the personalize_list role, which administrators have by default. With the role, you can hide controls and define access conditions (by role) for existing controls. </span></p><p><span style="color: #485563; font-family: arial; font-size: 13px; text-align: -webkit-auto;"><br/></span></p><h3>How to personalize a related list so the New and Edit buttons appear:</h3><p><span style="color: #485563; font-family: arial; font-size: 10pt; text-align: -webkit-auto;"><br/></span></p><ol><li style="text-align: -webkit-auto;">Open the form containing the related list to update. In the example, the <strong>Incident</strong> form is used.</li><li style="text-align: -webkit-auto;"><span style="color: #485563; font-family: arial; font-size: 13px; text-align: -webkit-auto;">Right-click the header bar of the related list and select </span><span style="color: #485563; font-family: arial; font-size: 13px; text-align: -webkit-auto;"><strong>Personalize &gt; List Control. </strong>In the example, the <strong>Task SLAs</strong> related list is used.</span><br/><span style="color: #485563; font-family: arial; font-size: 13px; text-align: -webkit-auto;"><a _jive_internal="true" href="/servlet/JiveServlet/showImage/38-3391-13158/new_edit_buttons.png"><img  alt="new_edit_buttons.png" class="jive-image image-3" height="664" src="6c00b739db5c1fc03eb27a9e0f9619dd.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 346px;" width="1189"/></a><br/></span></li><li style="text-align: -webkit-auto;">On the List Control form, the <strong>Omit new button</strong> and <strong>Omit edit button</strong> option are selected by default. Clear these two options.<br/><a _jive_internal="true" href="/servlet/JiveServlet/showImage/38-3391-13155/new_edit_buttons1.png"><img  alt="new_edit_buttons1.png" class="image-0 jive-image" height="382" src="518d45c6db9413043eb27a9e0f961948.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 283px;" width="836"/></a></li><li style="text-align: -webkit-auto;">When the <strong>Omit new button</strong> and <strong>Omit edit button</strong> options are cleared, the <strong>New roles</strong> and <strong>Edit roles</strong> options appear. <br/><a _jive_internal="true" href="/servlet/JiveServlet/showImage/38-3391-13156/new_edit_buttons2.png"><img  alt="new_edit_buttons2.png" class="image-1 jive-image" height="379" src="ea3d6c8adb185f048c8ef4621f9619af.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 280px;" width="838"/></a></li><li style="text-align: -webkit-auto;">[Optional] Select specific roles that can use the buttons. Click the lock icon next to <strong>New</strong> <strong>roles</strong> or <strong>Edit</strong> <strong>roles</strong> and move roles from the <strong>Available</strong> column to the <strong>Selected</strong> column.<br/><a _jive_internal="true" href="/servlet/JiveServlet/showImage/38-3391-13157/new_edit_buttons3.png"><img  alt="new_edit_buttons3.png" class="jive-image image-2" height="545" src="d5db844edb909704ed6af3231f9619cd.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 283px;" width="1194"/></a></li><li style="text-align: -webkit-auto;">Click <strong>Update</strong>.</li><li style="text-align: -webkit-auto;">On the parent form, check that the <strong>New</strong> and <strong>Edit</strong> buttons appear in the related list.</li></ol><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px; color: #485563; font-family: arial; font-size: 13px; text-align: -webkit-auto;">  </p><p>Step 3 is the spot that can be confusing. If you select the <strong>Omit new button</strong> and <strong>Omit edit button</strong>, the buttons do <em>not</em> appear on the related list.   If you want the buttons to appear and you want to control the roles that can use the buttons, be sure to clear the <strong>Omit new button</strong> and <strong>Omit edit button</strong> options.</p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p>To have Matthew walk you through these steps, view the <span style="text-decoration: underline;"><a class="jive-link-external-small" href="https://hi.service-now.com/kb_view.do?sysparm_article=KB0522170" rel="nofollow" target="_blank"><span style="color: #0000ff; text-decoration: underline;">UI Related List video tutorial</span></a></span>.</p>
