<p style="text-align: center;"><em>"Software is like entropy. It is difficult to grasp, weighs nothing, and obeys the second law of thermodynamics; i.e. it always increases."</em></p><p style="text-align: center;">-Norman Ralph Augustine</p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">If you are starting or revamping your asset management program, one of the first steps is to figure out what assets, including software assets, you have. How can you obtain information about what you own, what you lease, where assets are physically located, and who is using specific assets?</span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">For a great start, use the ServiceNow <a class="jive-link-external-small" href="http://wiki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Discovery" rel="nofollow" target="_blank"><span style="color: #0000ff; text-decoration: underline;">Discovery</span></a> application (or a third-party tool) to do the following:</span></p><ul><li><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">identify computers and devices connected to your network</span></li><li><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">update the CMDB with configuration, provisioning, and status information obtained about identified assets</span></li><li><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">identify software running on computers</span></li></ul><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p>In addition to Discovery, the <a class="jive-link-external-small" href="http://wiki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Asset_Management" rel="nofollow" target="_blank"><span style="color: #0000ff; text-decoration: underline;">Asset Management</span></a> application contains overview gauges, a list of assets in stock, and a list of unallocated software licenses that can also help you identify and manage assets.</p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><h3>Overview gauges</h3><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p>For a snazzy visual look at your assets, navigate to <strong>Asset Management &gt; Overview</strong>. In the example below, there are several helpful gauges showing licenses, configuration items, configuration item types, and computers listed by manufacturer.</p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p><a _jive_internal="true" href="/servlet/JiveServlet/showImage/38-3694-16101/asset_identification1.png"><img  alt="asset_identification1.png" class="image-0 jive-image" height="704" src="24190c02db1c1304b322f4621f9619f6.iix" style="height: 517px; width: 620px;" width="845"/></a></p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p>You can customize the Asset Management overview page to change the layout and show the information important to you. For example, I started with the overview page shown above and in just a few minutes, I created this custom page with a new layout and different information:</p><p><a _jive_internal="true" href="/servlet/JiveServlet/showImage/38-3694-16106/asset_identification2.png"><img  alt="asset_identification2.png" class="image-0 jive-image" height="658" src="b1a82b35dbd41fc068c1fb651f9619ae.iix" style="height: 335px; width: 620px;" width="1217"/></a></p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><h3>Assets in stock</h3><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p>View a list of all assets that are in stock by doing one of the following:</p><ul><li>If you are on the Dublin release or an earlier version, navigate to <strong>Asset Management &gt; Stock &gt; In Stock</strong></li><li>If you are on the Eureka release or later, navigate to <strong>Inventory Management &gt; Stock &gt; In Stock</strong></li></ul><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p>The list of in-stock assets also shows other useful information including the <strong>Asset</strong> tag, the <strong>Model category</strong>, the <strong>Display name</strong>, and the <strong>Configuration Item</strong>.</p><p><a _jive_internal="true" href="/servlet/JiveServlet/showImage/38-3694-16109/asset_identification3.png"><img  alt="asset_identification3.png" class="jive-image image-2" height="596" src="b459cdcadb90d344e9737a9e0f96195c.iix" style="height: 351px; width: 620px;" width="1052"/></a></p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><h3>Unallocated software licenses</h3><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p>Software is a tricky asset to manage. It is especially frustrating (not to mention, expensive) to have software licenses that are unallocated and not being used. You can quickly identify these unallocated licenses using the Software Asset Management application and the Asset Management application. After you have a list of unallocated licenses, consider doing any or all of the following:</p><ul><li>allocate the licenses to individuals</li><li>allocate the licenses to computers</li><li>order fewer copies (or none at all) of the software in the future</li></ul><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p>To check that the Software Asset Management plugin is activated:</p><ol><li><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">Navigate to </span><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"><strong>System Definition &gt; Plugins</strong></span><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">.</span></li><li>In the <strong>Name</strong> column, navigate to <strong>Software Asset Management</strong>.</li><li>In the <strong>Status</strong> column, check if the plugin is active. If it is <strong>Inactive</strong>, go to the next step.</li><li>In the <strong>Name</strong> column, click <strong>Software Asset Management</strong>.</li><li><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">Click </span><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"><strong>Activate/Upgrade</strong></span><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">.</span></li><li>Click <strong>Activate</strong>.</li></ol><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p>To view a list of unallocated software licenses:</p><ol><li><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">Do one of the following:</span></li></ol><ul><li>If you are on the Dublin release or an earlier version, navigate to <strong>Asset Management &gt; Stock &gt; Unallocated licenses</strong></li><li>If you are on the Eureka release or later, navigate to <strong>Asset Management &gt; Software &gt; Unallocated Licenses</strong></li></ul><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p>The unallocated licenses are listed, along with other useful information such as <strong>Model</strong>, <strong>Location</strong>, <strong>Cost center</strong>, and <strong>Valuation</strong>.</p><p><a _jive_internal="true" href="/servlet/JiveServlet/showImage/38-3694-16115/asset_identification4.png"><img  alt="asset_identification4.png" class="image-0 jive-image" height="239" src="1c356446db905f048c8ef4621f9619c7.iix" style="height: 132px; width: 620px;" width="1125"/></a></p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;">ServiceNow offers more information about identifying assets.</p><p style="min-height: 8pt;
