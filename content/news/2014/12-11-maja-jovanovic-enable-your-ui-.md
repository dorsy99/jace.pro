<p>A User Interface (UI) Policy is a rule that applies to a form that dynamically changes the information presented on it. UI Policies are a great alternative to using client scripts. Once you save the UI Policy a new UI Policy Actions is created in the Related List displays. Since Eureka, we have seen a flood of questions and incidents regarding UI policies failing. In most cases the cause of policy failure was because of the <a title="k-external-small" class="jive-link-external-small" href="http://wiki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Condition_Builder#Condition_Builder_Format" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">condition builder</a>. In some of our earlier releases you could add in a Condition builder field that was not on a form and a UI Policy would still work, but this is no more.</p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><h3>Enable your UI Policy to work on a form:</h3><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p>An example of this issue is would be: On the incident form, we have a field called "Incident state" (incident_state) and by default this field is not visible on the form. If you make a UI Policy with the condition that<strong> "Incident state" "is"   "Active"</strong> true, it will return with nothing.</p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p>As mentioned in the product documentation regarding <a title="k-external-small" class="jive-link-external-small" href="http://wiki.servicenow.com/index.php?title=Creating_a_UI_Policy" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">Creating a UI Policy</a>, the field has to be on the form for the UI policy to work.</p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p>How to add the incident state on the form.</p><ol><li>Navigate to<strong> </strong>the<strong> Incident</strong></li><li>Right-click the header</li><li>Click<strong> Personalize,</strong> then<strong> Form Layout</strong>.</li><li>Select the Incident state field <span style="font-size: 13.63636302948px;">that you created</span> from the slushbucket</li><li>Click the <strong>Add</strong> button to move it to the <strong>Selected</strong> column</li><li>Click <strong>Save</strong>.</li></ol><p><a _jive_internal="true" href="/servlet/JiveServlet/showImage/38-3709-16267/personalize form.png"><img  alt="personalize form.png" class="image-1 jive-image" height="900" src="0535e44edbdc5304b322f4621f961928.iix" style="border: 1px solid #e6e6e6; height: 381px; width: 620px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="1464"/></a></p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p>It is important here to keep in mind that the policy isn't failing or causing the error in a log or console. <strong>This behavior is by design and when field isn't on the form the condition simply reverts to false.</strong> The s<span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">ame behavior is applied for a dot-walked field. </span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;"><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; line-height: 1.5em;">An example of a dot-walked field on an Incident would be Caller.Company or Caller.City.</span></p><p><a _jive_internal="true" href="/servlet/JiveServlet/showImage/38-3709-16268/dot walked field.png"><img  alt="dot walked field.png" class="jive-image image-2" height="749" src="ca24cc4edb1cd3049c9ffb651f96197d.iix" style="border: 1px solid #e6e6e6; height: 290px; width: 620px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="1600"/></a></p><p style="text-align: center;">**Even if this behavior was allowed in some of our previous releases that is no longer available in Eureka**</p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px; text-align: center;">  </p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px; text-align: center;">  </p><p style="text-align: left;">If you are on Eureka and you notice that your UI Policy is not working, be sure to check that the field is available on the form.</p>
