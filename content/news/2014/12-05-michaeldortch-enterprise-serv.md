<p><span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;">Call it "Alphabet Soup for the Soul of the Enterprise:"</span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;"><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;">"ITSM + GRC = ESPE," where:</span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;"><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;">"ITSM" = "IT service management;"</span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;"><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;">"GRC" = "governance, risk, and compliance;" and</span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;"><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;">"ESPE" = "enterprise service and process excellence."</span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;"><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;">That last FLA ("four-letter acronym," of course!) may be new to you, but its underlying concepts should not be. They are, in fact, the ultimate goal of every enterprise, regardless of its size or primary activities. Why? Because every enterprise is defined by its most critical services and the processes that enable and underlie them.</span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;"><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;">This means that to survive, let alone to thrive competitively, every enterprise must be focused on delivery and management of consistently excellent IT and business services, to its customers, partners, and internal and external users. Success in these efforts, in turn, absolutely requires proven, effective processes, consistently applied and executed across the enterprise.</span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;"><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;">And since every critical enterprise service and process relies upon IT, another critical success factor is integrated, automated, and optimized ITSM. Such ITSM is also critical to success with GRC, within and beyond IT. (If you aren't yet convinced about the value of and need for effective GRC, please read or re-read my earlier post, </span><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em;">"</span><a title="" _jive_internal="true" data-containerid="2927" data-containertype="37" data-objectid="3404" data-objecttype="38" href="/community?id=community_blog&sys_id=d78d2a69dbd0dbc01dcaf3231f96195d">Why Your Enterprise Can't — and Shouldn't — Spell 'GRC' Without 'ITSM</a><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em;">.'")</span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;"><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;">To deliver consistently excellent services, your enterprise needs consistently excellent processes. And to deliver consistently excellent processes, your enterprise needs consistently excellent, well-integrated ITSM and GRC oversight.</span></p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><h2><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">Enterprise Service and Process Excellence: Of Roadmaps and Beer</span></h2><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;"><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;">So what does your enterprise need to achieve these worthy, essential goals?</span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;"><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;">The same thing every navigator needs to reach a desired destination safely: a <strong>roadmap</strong>. In this context, that roadmap is a plan with specific goals and destinations, specific routes to success, and even specific "off ramps" and alternate routes in case of the equivalents of speed bumps, traffic jams, and major accidents. Every enterprise simply must create, then successfully navigate a roadmap to ESPE, via integrated, automated, and optimized ITSM and GRC oversight.</span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;"><br/></span></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;">And what do you need to craft such a roadmap and the ability to navigate it successfully? Heck, that's as easy as beer. After all, beer only has four ingredients — water, grains, hops, and yeast. And like beer, success with your ESPE roadmap only requires four ingredients.</span></p><ul><li><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 1.5em; font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif;">Clear, specific business goals and priorities.</span></li><li><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">Consistent, sustained executive buy-in and support of ESPE efforts.</span></li><li><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">The political will and leadership needed to assess incumbent processes, policies, people, and solutions accurately and fairly, and to change or remove those not sufficiently aligned with goals and priorities.</span></li><li><span style="font-family: calibri, verdana, arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">Proven, experienced help.
