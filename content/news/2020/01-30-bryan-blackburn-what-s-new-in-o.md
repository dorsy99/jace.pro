<h3>Software Asset Management </h3>
<h4>SAM Vulnerability Assessment </h4>
<p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">If there is unknown software being used on devices you can’t identify, it can feel nearly impossible to guarantee a secure environment. With SAM and SecOps working together, you can help identify and remediate vulnerable software at your company with automated workflows, helping you operate and secure your IT environment. SAM works with information from the National Vulnerability Database (NVD) to identify which versions of software have known vulnerabilities. SAM, leveraging a vast Content Library, normalizes potentially applicable discovery models. The pattern variations identified are critical for completeness when remediating the vulnerable versions. SAM NVD Vulnerability Assessment combines this information to track vulnerabilities within your system by creating vulnerable items ready for action. </span></p>
<h4>SaaS License Connections </h4>
<p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">ServiceNow SAM offers deep usage and spend analysis for high-value, ubiquitous applications including Box, DropBox, DocuSign, G-Suite, Salesforce Sales Cloud, Salesforce Service Cloud, Zoom, Adobe Creative Cloud, Office 365, Webex, and Jira.  </span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">With SaaS License Connections, you can create custom integrations to gain insights into any SaaS app in your environment that has an API. Leverage Flow Designer and IntegrationHub to build your own basic, low-code integration for extended visibility into how your licenses are being used and where savings opportunities might exist. </span></p>

<h4>SaaS License Management (enhancements) </h4>
<p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">SaaS License Management made its General Availability debut in New York, enabling our customers to detect, analyze, and optimize SaaS across the business. We’ve added Webex and Jira to our list of publishers for which we offer deep insight for usage and cost.</span>  </p>
<h5 style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Webex </span></h5>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12pt;">With usage data available for Cisco Webex Meetings, see how many licenses are being used against what has been purchased. You’ll have the data you need to make purchasing decisions like whether to buy more licenses mid-contract or to reclaim and repurpose stale licenses. We analyze last hosted meeting dates to determine licenses that are stale against your determined threshold date.   </span></p>
<h5 style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Jira </span></h5>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12pt;">We analyze Jira usage based on users creating issues and any activity shown in the audit log. Define your own activity threshold to reclaim Jira licenses that have gone stale with no issue creation and no activity in the audit log.</span></p>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;"> </p>
<h4>Engineering License Manager </h4>
<p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Engineering License Manager helps manage and optimize licenses for thousands of specialty software types through a dozen types of license servers for industrial and engineering applications.  This feature provides visibility into the overall engineering software license position, helping overcome challenges such as audit risk, overspending, inefficient usage of licenses, and inaccurate forecasting.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">The industry-leading <a href="https://store.servicenow.com/sn_appstore_store.do#!/store/home" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow">OpenLM integration</a> helps complete the engineering software inventory, connecting to a variety of license management servers to feed usage data directly into the software asset management lifecycle. Engineering License Manager helps provide a clearer view of what licenses are owned, how they are being used, and helps identify cost-reduction opportunities. You can monitor concurrent license consumption, track and reduce software spend, avoid productivity losses, and gain data-rich reporting on license activity, usage, alerts, and accurate denials. See what it looks like in this <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v&#61;XpFsFwbFeek" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow">video</a>. </span></p>
<p> </p>
<h4>Software Maintenance </h4>
<p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Software Maintenance provides visibility into software maintenance contracts and helps optimize maintenance spend. Without visibility, you may be renewing contracts as the path of least resistance, which often leads to over expenditures. When you are paying maintenance on licenses that aren’t being used, these costs accumulate. Software Maintenance enables you to right-size maintenance spend and cancel maintenance for unused licenses—ensuring maintenance costs are limited only to licenses being used.  </span></p>
<p> </p>
<h4>Automatic license metric assignments </h4>
<p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Software with publisher part numbers are automatically assigned licensed metrics.  Save a ton of publisher research and get to license positions faster by utilizing the embedded license metric intelligence and Content Library. </span></p>
<p> </p>
<h4>Office 365 Publisher Pack (enhancements) </h4>
<h5 style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Reserve Licenses </span></h5>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12pt;">This enhancement helps track Office 365 reserve licenses with the ability to reserve available subscriptions throughout the contractual period. You can gain insight into the total number of subscriptions purchased and calculate accurate true-up costs for reserve licenses. </span></p>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;"> </p>
<h4>Oracle Infrastructure Report </h4>
<p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Provides a specific report required for Oracle audits that often require the collection of information such as database versions deployed, underlying hardware and virtual machine Information, virtualization technologies or license metrics for each database instance. </span></p>
<p> </p>
<h4>Entitlement import enhancements </h4>
<p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Additional capabilities have been added to account for specific use cases around database options for Oracle database software, importing contracts, referencing existing contracts, or importing custom, user-defined fields.</span></p>
<p> </p>
<h3>Asset Management  </h3>
<h4>For Mobile </h4>
<h5>Mobile My Assets, Incident Reporting </h5>
<p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">Mobile My Assets allows the end-user to request assets, view assets they are assigned, and open an incident for that asset in our New York release.</span> </p>
<p> </p>
<h5>Mobile Asset Receiving (Multi-scan) </h5>
<p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">IT agents can receive an asset by scanning barcodes with a mobile iOS or Android device. Assets are received against open Purchase Orders; a receiving slip can also be attached via a mobile camera. Asset records are automatically updated from mobile workflow and received to location stockroom. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 12pt;">With Mobile Asset Receiving Multi-scan, IT fulfillers can receive multiple assets against a Purchase Order in a single process on the mobile agent app. Users scan barcodes with a mobile iOS or Android device to reduce manual data entry and risk of error. </span></p>
