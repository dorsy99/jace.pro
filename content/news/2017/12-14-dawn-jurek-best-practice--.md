<p>Following on the heels of our <a title="Best practices for implementing the Change Management application" __default_attr="7299" __jive_macro_name="blogpost" class="jive_macro jive_macro_blogpost" data-orig-content="Best practices for implementing the Change Management application" data-renderedposition="9.815340995788574_192.3295440673828_452_16" href="/community?id=community_blog&sys_id=e4edeae9dbd0dbc01dcaf3231f961962">Best practices for implementing the Change Management application</a>, we delve into standard change a bit more, as it's a great mechanism for automating common, low-risk requests. <span style="color: #666666; font-family: arial, sans-serif;">In this installment of our </span><a title="" _jive_internal="true" data-containerid="2927" data-containertype="37" data-objectid="7090" data-objecttype="38" href="/community?id=community_blog&sys_id=6e7d6269dbd0dbc01dcaf3231f9619c0" style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #3778c7;">NOWSupport best practices series</a>, we also show you how to create a standard change in the ServiceNow platform.</p><p></p><h2>Streamline your operations with standard changes</h2><p>In the ServiceNow system, a <em>standard change</em> is a low-risk change that's pre-approved. When a user submits a request for a standard change, it doesn't need approval by a live person—the system creates and assigns the change automatically.</p><p></p><p>Users request standard changes through a service catalog. The base system comes with a few standard changes, like rebooting a Windows server. As shown in our example below, a user with the itil role searches the service catalog for <strong>reboot Windows server</strong>...</p><p></p><p><img   alt="standard_change_service_catalog_search.png" class="image-1 jive-image" src="c87eb37ddb141304b322f4621f9619cd.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 323px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></p><p></p><p>...clicks on the link in the <strong>Catalog Search Results</strong>,...</p><p style="text-align: center;"><img   alt="standard_change_service_catalog_search_result.png" class="image-3 jive-image" src="29d2898edbd09f048c8ef4621f9619bb.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 193px;"/></p><p></p><p>...and schedules the reboot via the <strong>Change Request</strong> form.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><img   alt="standard_change_reboot_Windows_server.png" class="image-2 jive-image" src="4e02e546dbdcd344e9737a9e0f9619b3.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 451px;"/></p><p></p><p>Standard changes can be a great way to streamline your operations. Since they're pre-approved, they save the time and effort required for manual approval—every time. This lets your change management team and change fulfillers focus on changes with higher risk and impact.</p><p></p><p>To make the most of this feature, take a look at the changes your organization makes often. Any change that's low-risk and is typically approved and completed successfully without issues is a good candidate to be a standard change. The more standard changes you create, the more time and effort you'll save.</p><p></p><h2>Activating Standard Change Catalog on your instance</h2><p>If the <strong>Standard Change</strong> Application doesn't appear in the navigator, you'll need to activate the <strong>Change Management — Standard Change Catalog</strong> plugin (com.snc.change_management.standard_change_catalog) on your instance to get this functionality. This applies mostly to customers who initially implemented prior to the Geneva release. To learn more about plugins, see our post<a title="How do you plug in a plugin? The ins and outs of plugin activation" __default_attr="6491" __jive_macro_name="blogpost" class="jive_macro jive_macro_blogpost" data-orig-content="How do you plug in a plugin? The ins and outs of plugin activation" data-renderedposition="1568.7357177734375_249.4176025390625_431_16" href="/community?id=community_blog&sys_id=8f2e2a6ddbd0dbc01dcaf3231f9619e0">How do you plug in a plugin? The ins and outs of plugin activation</a>.</p><pre __default_attr="info" __jive_macro_name="alert" alert="info" class="jive_text_macro jive_macro_alert" data-renderedposition="1601.818115234375_7.997159004211426_1192_43"><p>Note that only users with the admin role can activate plugins.</p></pre><h3>To activate the Change Management — Standard Change Catalog plugin:</h3><ol><ol><li>Navigate to <strong>System Definition &gt; Plugins</strong>.</li><li>In the Search field, enter <strong>change management — standard change catalog</strong>.</li><li>Click the <strong>Change Management — Standard Change Catalog</strong> link.</li><li>Under Related Links, click <strong>Activate/Upgrade.</strong></li></ol></ol><p><img   alt="standard_change_plugin.png" class="image-5 jive-image" src="388cd4cedb9c17049c9ffb651f96195c.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 229px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></p><p>After you activate the plugin, you should test prior to implementation per your organization's procedures. For more information about activating the standard change catalog, see the product documentation page for your release. Here's the one for Helsinki: <a title="ocs.servicenow.com/bundle/helsinki-it-service-management/page/product/change-management/task/t_ActivateStandardChangeCatalog.html" href="https://docs.servicenow.com/bundle/helsinki-it-service-management/page/product/change-management/task/t_ActivateStandardChangeCatalog.html">Activate standard change catalog</a>. Also, see the product documentation for <a title="ocs.servicenow.com/bundle/helsinki-it-service-management/page/product/change-management/task/migrate-change-mgmt-pre-geneva.html" href="https://docs.servicenow.com/bundle/helsinki-it-service-management/page/product/change-management/task/migrate-change-mgmt-pre-geneva.html">considerations when migrating to Standard Change</a> as well as a <a title="ocs.servicenow.com/bundle/helsinki-it-service-management/page/product/change-management/reference/r_InstlldWthStdChgCat.html" href="https://docs.servicenow.com/bundle/helsinki-it-service-management/page/product/change-management/reference/r_InstlldWthStdChgCat.html">detailed list of what is installed with Standard Change</a>.</p><p></p><h2>Creating a standard change</h2><p>Your organization can define additional standard changes to suit your operations. To create a new standard change, you'll create a <em>standard change proposal</em>. You need the <strong>itil</strong> or <strong>change manager</strong> role to do this.   The procedure is different for each role, as described below.</p><p></p><h3>To create a standard change proposal as a user with the change manager role:</h3><ol><li>Navigate to <strong>Change &gt; Standard Change &gt; My Proposals</strong>.</li><li>Click <strong>New</strong>.</li></ol><p></p><p>The Standard Change Proposal form lets you specify the details of the change itself and the accompanying change request template for evaluation by the change management team.</p><p><img   alt="propose_standard_change_template_itil_user.png" class="image-7 jive-image" src="53824142db5897041dcaf3231f96198c.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 419px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></p><p></p><h3>To create a standard change proposal as a user with the itil role:</h3><ol><li>Navigate to <strong>Change &gt; Standard Change &gt; Standard Change Catalog</strong>.</li><li>Click <strong>Template Management</strong>.</li><li>Click <strong>Propose a new Standard Change Template</strong>.</li></ol><pre __default_attr="info" __jive_macro_name="alert" alert="info" class="jive_text_macro jive_macro_alert" data-renderedposition="2894.801025390625_7.997159004211426_1192_44"><p>Note that the form may differ from that shown below depending on the form view of the logged-in user.</p></pre><p><img   alt="propose_standard_change_template.png" class="image-6 jive-image" src="4b256dcadb50d7049c9ffb651f9619af.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 450px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></p><p></p><p>For either procedure, when you complete the form and request approval, the system submits the proposal to the change management team for review. Once the proposal is approved, the system automatically adds the new standard change template to the catalog. That's it—now the change can be created and assigned quickly from the catalog without the need for review and approval by the change management team.</p><p></p><p>Ready to get stared with standard change?
