<p>At a time when Twitter, Facebook, Google and Yahoo give you flexibility to select what you want and don't want to read, the email is still one of the places where "unsubscribe" is not always an option. As users go on holidays, move jobs (e.g. the account get disabled), mailboxes get full, emails are marked as spam, etc, some email relays would start bouncing back those emails, and those users would not be able to unsubscribe to those senders. On your instance, it is a good practice to review the emails bounced back and review the users for which no further notification should be sent. This requires an efficient and regular intervention, as bounced emails would slowly increase if no action is taken. From the administration point of view, if users are empower themselves to unsubscribe, you will have less work to do, and wanted emails would go out faster.</p><p><img   alt="bounce-mail.-e1478601620490x.png" class="image-8 jive-image" height="371" src="9ed6d482dbd817049c9ffb651f9619db.iix" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; width: 399px; height: 370.775px;" width="399"/></p><p></p><p>Following up my previous blog <a title="Speed up your email delivery by validating recipients" __default_attr="5296" __jive_macro_name="blogpost" class="jive_macro jive_macro_blogpost" data-orig-content="Speed up your email delivery by validating recipients" data-renderedposition="519.765625_202.984375_348_16" href="/community?id=community_blog&sys_id=932e6a6ddbd0dbc01dcaf3231f961953">Speed up your email delivery by validating recipients</a>, here is my review on the matter.</p><p></p><h1>3 ways to minimize bounced emails</h1><ol><li><span style="color: #3d3d3d;">Educate your users to unsubscribe from the unwanted notifications instead of just ignoring them</span></li><li><span style="color: #3d3d3d;">Regularly execute a validation of the em</span>ails bounced back to the instance</li><li>Archive junk emails to improve the email table performance</li></ol><p></p><h2>Educate your users to unsubscribe from unwanted notifications</h2><p>To educate your users to unsubscribe, ensure to inform them this functionality is available on your instance. Users can create filters within their preference to only receive the notification if the condition set on their personalised filter are met. They could also choose to disable per notification, or disable notifications received by the user altogether. This does not affect the user active status - just the notifications targeted to them.</p><p></p><p>Here is one example from a notifications received by one user: angelo.ferentz.</p><p><img   alt="2017-09-30_1045-email-recieved.png" class="image-1 jive-image" height="446" src="2dd6d046dbd41f048c8ef4621f96196b.iix" style="width: 601px; height: 445.903px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="601"/></p><p></p><p>For this, users can go to their<a title="ocs.servicenow.com/bundle/jakarta-servicenow-platform/page/administer/notification/concept/user-notification-preferences.html" href="https://docs.servicenow.com/bundle/jakarta-servicenow-platform/page/administer/notification/concept/user-notification-preferences.html"> notification preferences</a>, then select the notification they have received and set the dial to "off." That would avoid further notifications from being sent to this user only.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><img   alt="2017-09-30_1045-setting-off.png" class="image-2 jive-image" src="b47e15c6db54d7041dcaf3231f961927.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 326px;"/></p><p></p><p>They can also decide to leave them ON, but set a <a title="ocs.servicenow.com/bundle/jakarta-servicenow-platform/page/administer/notification/task/apply-notification-conditions.html#apply-notification-conditions" href="https://docs.servicenow.com/bundle/jakarta-servicenow-platform/page/administer/notification/task/apply-notification-conditions.html#apply-notification-conditions">personalised filter</a> that avoids them under certain conditions.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><img   alt="2017-09-30_1050-advanced-filter.png" class="image-3 jive-image" src="8bc97082db1497041dcaf3231f96199e.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 315px;"/></p><p></p><p></p><p>Finally, if users decide to stop any notification from being sent to their devices (e.g. before going on holiday), users can select to disable them altogether.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><img   alt="2017-09-30_1107-disable-all.png" class="image-4 jive-image" src="92375c4adbd41f048c8ef4621f96194c.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 320px;"/></p><p></p><p>From the performance point of view, if users only enable the notifications they require, there would be less emails to send. This is a much better option than generating tons of emails that go out and back again, when these settings are ignored.</p><pre __default_attr="success" __jive_macro_name="alert" alert="success" class="jive_text_macro jive_macro_alert" data-renderedposition="2573.84375_8_1177_43"><p>If you are reading this, unsubscribe of all the unwanted notifications   to help improve performance on the services you receive and provide.</p></pre><p></p><h2>Regularly execute a validation of the emails bounced back to the instance</h2><p>There is not standard practice to parse the emails bounced back. This is especially important if you depend on emails as very large tables tend to degrade over time and delay upgrades.</p><p></p><p>As per performance, we are looking for email retention rules: <strong>"Emails - Ignored and over 90 days old"</strong>. This rule archives email message records that were created more than 90 days prior to the current date and are of type received-ignored or sent-ignored.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><img   alt="2017-09-30_1326-archiving.png" class="image-6 jive-image" src="6490e186db94d7041dcaf3231f961991.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 457px;"/></p><p></p><p></p><p>Once the emails are archived, then they stay on the archive for a year before being destroyed.</p><p style="text-align: center;"><img   alt="2017-09-30_1331-destroying.png" class="image-7 jive-image" src="a8183cc2dbd097041dcaf3231f96190d.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 204px;"/></p><p></p><p>Now, it is time to put this all in place and allow your users to unsubscribe. For those which the notifications bounce back, then set the sys_user 'notification' to <strong>Disabled</strong>. Finally, for those emails ending up on your instance Junk, archive and destroy them.
