font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">1.   Navigate to System Applications &gt; Studio.   A second browser tab will open with the studio Load Application form.</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">2.   Click on the Create Application button.   The Create Application form will be displayed.</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">3.   Click on the Create button for <strong>Start from scratch</strong>.</span></p><p></p><p><img   class="image-1 jive-image" height="180" src="50efa48edb9c57041dcaf3231f96198a.iix" style="max-width: 1200px; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">1.   Click on the Create Application File button</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">2.   In the Filter field type Client Script, and choose Client Script from the Filter Results</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">3.   Fill out the form with the following:</span></p><p></p><p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;"><strong>Name</strong></span><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">: Logging Scoped</span></p><p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;"><strong>Table</strong></span><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">: Incident</span></p><p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;"><strong>Active</strong></span><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">: True</span></p><p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;"><strong>UI Type</strong></span><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">: Desktop</span></p><p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;"><strong>Type</strong></span><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">: onLoad</span></p><p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;"><strong>Description</strong></span><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">: Script to test logging and debugging from the browser.</span></p><p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;"><strong>Script</strong></span><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; Incident Number: ' + number);</span></p><p style="margin-bottom: .0001pt;"><span style="font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">}</span></p></pre><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">4. Click the Submit button to save your script.</span></p><p></p><p><img   class="jive-image image-4" src="a870958edb181b04ed6af3231f9619e3.iix" style="max-width: 1200px; max-height: 900px;"/></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">5. From your Instance navigate to Incident &gt; Open</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">6. Open your favorite incident.   The alert statement will fire and should look something like this:</span></p><p></p><p><img   class="image-5 jive-image" height="116" src="2d8d20c2db18dfc068c1fb651f96191b.iix" style="width: 354px; height: 115.906px;" width="355"/></p><p></p><p style="margin-bottom: .0001pt;"><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">Alert provides a way for a developer to indicate how far the code has executed on the Browser side.   I usually do not use this for production code.   Instead I find g_form.addErrorMessage or g_form.addInfoMessage to be better more user-friendly mechanisms. I find myself using alert less-and-less, and instead I use jslog().   jslog() places the statement in the console log instead of interrupting the user with a modal pop-up box!   This is a much preferred method; especially if you forget to remove the log statement before pushing to production!   BTW, an excellent article on what is, and is not, allowed in Scoped client-side scripts is: <a href="https://docs.servicenow.com/bundle/istanbul-servicenow-platform/page/build/service-portal/concept/unsupported_client_scripts.html" title="https://docs.servicenow.com/bundle/istanbul-servicenow-platform/page/build/service-portal/concept/unsupported_client_scripts.html">Supported and unsupported client scripts</a>. </span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-family: Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">7. In the Studio modify the Logging Scoped Client Script. Change the alert statement to a jslog() statement.</span></p><p></p><p style="margin-bottom: 0.0001pt; padding-left: 30px;"><span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;"><strong>Script</strong>:</span></p><p></p><pre __default_attr="javascript" __jive_macro_name="code" class="jive_macro_code _jivemacro_uid_14924408153281975 jive_text_macro" data-renderedposition="3136.178955078125_7.997159004211426_1176_93" jivemacro_uid="_14924408153281975"><p style="margin-bottom: .0001pt;"><span style="font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">function onLoad() {</span></p><p></p><p style="margin-bottom: .0001pt;"><span style="font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">                       var number = g_form.getValue('number');</span></p><p style="margin-bottom: .0001pt;"><span style="font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">                       jslog('---&gt; Incident Number: ' + number);</span></p><p style="margin-bottom: .0001pt;"><span style="font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">                       </span></p><p style="margin-bottom: .0001pt;"><span style="font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">}</span></p></pre><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">8. Click on the Update button to save your script modification.   This will automatically place a log message into the console log when we open an incident record.</span></p><p></p><p style="margin-bottom: .0001pt;"><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">Okay, we now have everything we need to begin testing and debugging.</span></p><p></p><p></p><p style="margin-bottom: .0001pt;"></p><p style="margin-bottom: .0001pt;"><span style="font-size: 16.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;"><strong>Client Side Debugging Tools</strong></span></p><p></p><p style="margin-bottom: .0001pt;"><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: Arial, sans-serif;"><strong>The JavaScript Log and Field Watcher</strong></span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">7. Open an incident record, or if you still have it open from the previous testing; right click at the top of the incident form and click on the Reload Form link.   The idea is to trigger the onLoad Client Scripts.   The alert box should no longer be displayed.</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">8. From your instance click on the Systems Settings gear button in the upper right.   This will open the System Settings form. </span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">9. Click on the Developer tab.</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">10. Make sure the following switches are set to "on":</span></p><p></p><ul style="list-style-type: disc;"><li><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">Show application picker in header</span></li><li><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">Show update set picker in header</span></li><li><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">JavaScript Log and Field Watcher</span><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">                   </span></li></ul><p></p><p style="margin-bottom: .0001pt;"><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">As a Scoped Application developer I like having the first two turned on to allow me to quickly jump between scopes.   This also allows me to make sure I am in the right Scope AND the right Update Set.   The JavaScript Log and Field Watcher allows me to view the console log specific to ServiceNow, and it will be where our jslog messages will appear.   BTW, I don't use <a title="ocs.servicenow.com/bundle/istanbul-servicenow-platform/page/script/debugging/task/t_UsingFieldWatcher.html" href="https://docs.servicenow.com/bundle/istanbul-servicenow-platform/page/script/debugging/task/t_UsingFieldWatcher.html">Field Watcher</a> that much and won't be covering it in this article, but you might want to check it out and see if it is useful to you.</span></p><p></p><p><img   class="image-6 jive-image" src="d02d0c0adb5c1344e9737a9e0f9619d7.iix" style="max-width: 1200px; max-height: 900px;"/></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">11. At the bottom of the form make sure that <strong>JavaScript Log</strong> is selected, and click on the <strong>Medium</strong> button.   You may look at <strong>Small</strong> and <strong>Large</strong> just to get a feel for what they do.</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">12. Click on the <strong>Clear Log</strong> button (looks like a circle with a diagonal slash through it).   This will clear the immediate console log.   </span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">13. You may have to scroll down a bit in the JavaScript Log to find your jslog() message.   It should look something like this:</span></p><p></p><p><img   class="image-7 jive-image" src="1e1b2b71db149b048c8ef4621f9619d2.iix" style="max-width: 1200px; max-height: 900px;"/></p><p></p><p></p><p style="margin-bottom: .0001pt;"><span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: Arial, sans-serif;"><strong>Browser Developer Tools</strong></span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">An alternative to the ServiceNow JavaScript Log is your browser's console log.   Most every browser available has some sort of developer tool(s) that allows for this.   These tools display a lot more information about the state of your session than does the ServiceNow JavaScript Logger.   That can be good and bad.   Good in that all errors and warnings are displayed.   Bad in that there are a lot of messages that are displayed that are not ServiceNow specific.   This "noise" can be overwhelming when you first start using this method.   I will briefly cover three of the top browsers: Internet Explorer, Google Chrome, and Mozilla Firefox.</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">Note how in each browser the jslog message is displayed (the red arrows).</span></p><p></p><p style="margin-bottom: .0001pt;"><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">With <strong>Internet Explorer</strong> press the F12 button, and the console will be displayed.   Again you may have to scroll down a ways to see your jslog() message, but it will be there. There is no provision to dock it to the side like in other browsers.   However, you can undock it; at which point it will be in its own window which you can then move around or push to the back.   Click on the close ("x") button or press F12 again to remove the console.</span></p><p></p><p><img   class="jive-image image-8" src="02de85cadb1897049c9ffb651f961940.iix" style="max-width: 1200px; max-height: 900px;"/></p><p></p><p style="margin-bottom: .0001pt;"><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">With <strong>Google Chrome</strong> pressing the F12 button brings up the console.   Unlike Internet Explorer there is the ability to dock it to the side or at the bottom. Again you may have to scroll a bit or do a search to find your message.</span></p><p></p><p><img   class="image-9 jive-image" src="342d448edbdcdb048c8ef4621f9619f6.iix" style="max-width: 1200px; max-height: 900px;"/></p><p></p><p style="margin-bottom: .0001pt;"><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">Even though Chrome is my browser of choice I like the <strong>Mozilla FireFox</strong> <a title="eveloper.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Tools" href="https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Tools">developer tools</a> better for their organization and ease-of-use. Again, F12 brings up the console window, and like Chrome you can dock it to the side or at the bottom.   BTW, the old <a title="tfirebug.com/" href="http://getfirebug.com/">FireBug</a> plugin for FireFox has been deprecated.</span></p><p></p><p><img   class="jive-image image-10" src="8b1b2f71db981fc03eb27a9e0f961925.iix" style="max-width: 1200px; max-height: 900px;"/></p><p></p><p style="margin-bottom: .0001pt;"><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: 'Arial',sans-serif;">So that is pretty much it concerning the various client console logs and how to place log statements into them with Script.   </span></p><p style="margin-bottom: .0001pt;"></p><p style="margin-bottom: .0001pt;"><span style="font-size: 12.0pt;
