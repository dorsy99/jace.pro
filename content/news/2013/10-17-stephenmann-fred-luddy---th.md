<p>At the three recent EMEA NowForums ServiceNow founder and Chief Product Officer <a title="" _jive_internal="true" class="jiveTT-hover-user jive-link-profile-small" data-containerid="-1" data-containertype="-1" data-objectid="2005" data-objecttype="3" href="/community?id=community_user_profile&user=11409ae5db581fc09c9ffb651f9619ca">Fred Luddy</a> spoke of "The Future of Now." A mix of the future of IT and the future of ServiceNow — two futures that are inextricably linked, with ServiceNow's future in many ways being where our customers need us to be.</p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p>Fred started by saying that people say that the only thing we learn from history is that we don't learn anything from history; but there have been undeniable patterns:</p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><ol><li>Expect the unexpected</li><li>The hype cycle is real — irrational exuberance and the plateau of productivity</li><li>Unless companies stay agile, and adopt and adapt at the right time they may quickly become irrelevant.</li></ol><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p><strong>The IT "hot topics" of social, mobile, "big data," and cloud are still front and center</strong></p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p>The "nexus of forces" — as defined by global analyst firm Gartner — of social, mobile, analytics, and cloud will help define the future of IT (and business). But so will the expectations and needs of employees and customers.</p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p style="text-align: center;"><a _jive_internal="true" href="/servlet/JiveServlet/showImage/38-1342-2202/undeniably.png"><img  alt="" class="jive-image" height="287" src="1eb0210edb1c130468c1fb651f96195f.iix" width="500"/></a></p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p>And looking at cloud in particular:</p><p><br/><a _jive_internal="true" href="/servlet/JiveServlet/showImage/38-1342-2196/applicability.png"><img  alt="" class="jive-image" height="282" src="1443e586db1cd304b322f4621f96198b.iix" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="500"/></a></p><p>Cloud from a Core perspective can provide competitive differentiation and business value (Sales, Marketing, Development). But there is also a Context perspective which supports Core that is focused on operational efficiency. Quoting <a title="k-external-small" class="jive-link-external-small" href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z-z8_DB3Hy8" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">Geoffrey Moore</a>, while an organization will get no credit for doing Context well if "you screw it up you will be in deep doo doo."</p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p><strong>ServiceNow's continued evolution</strong></p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p>There's the scalability evolution:</p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><ul><li>Multiple app nodes</li><li>Sharding and archiving</li><li>Read replicas.</li></ul><p><br/>And the platform evolution:</p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><ul><li>High availability and disaster recovery mirrors</li><li>NoSQL with MongoDB.</li></ul><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><center><a _jive_internal="true" href="/servlet/JiveServlet/showImage/38-1342-2200/evolution.png"><img  alt="" class="jive-image" height="288" src="0031e4cedb9c1f048c8ef4621f961949.iix" width="500"/></a></center><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p><strong>Then there is the "use case" evolution</strong></p><p style="min-height: 8pt; height: 8pt; padding: 0px;">  </p><p>Fred spoke of how the ServiceNow IT Service Automation Suite and platform are being used in non-IT situations — to support the management of service relationships in the enterprise. More information can be found in a previous blog — <a title="k-external-small" class="jive-link-external-small" href="http://community.servicenow.com/blog/stephenmann/future-role-it-managing-service-relationships-enterprise" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">A Future Role for IT: Managing Service Relationships in the Enterprise</a> — with CERN, the European Organization for Nuclear Research, a great example of sharing what the IT organization has learned about managing service delivery to benefit other business functions, their customers, and ultimately the enterprise.</p><center><br/><a _jive_internal="true" href="/servlet/JiveServlet/showImage/38-1342-2199/cern.png"><img  alt="" class="jive-image" height="295" src="d34ebc42dbd497041dcaf3231f9619a8.iix" width="500"/></a></center><p><br/>CERN has taken the "IT" out of "ITSM" to apply service management best practices to waste, fire services, people, transport, and green space management — the infographic shows the activity in the first 12 weeks of 2013.
