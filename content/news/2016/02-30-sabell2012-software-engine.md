<p><span style="line-height: 1.5; font-size: 12pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">I thought I would share this as something of importance.   I know as ServiceNow software developers we do not build what could be termed necessarily as life-critical-systems (failure of which could cause loss-of-life); that I am aware of, but we do build software that if done poorly could result in loss of money.   </span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">For several years now ACM (<a title="w.acm.org/" href="http://www.acm.org/">Association for Computing Machinery</a>) has been tracking Software Risks-to-the-Public via the Software Engineering Special Interest Group (<a title="w.sigsoft.org/" href="http://www.sigsoft.org/">SIGSOFT</a>).   I received my copy once-a-month for several years, and it made me a believer.   No matter how small the system you work on; always keep in mind risks to the user base, and failure recovery.   </span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">The link below is for a cumulative article published as of early last year, by Peter Neumann of <a title="ww.sri.com/" href="https://www.sri.com/">SRI International</a> (Stanford U.) in Menlo Park.   It is in essence everything from the ACM publications, but also covers hardware problems.   Take special notice of the Descriptor Symbols section.   Then read on.   It is amazing what is listed.</span></p><p></p><p><span style="font-size: 12pt;
