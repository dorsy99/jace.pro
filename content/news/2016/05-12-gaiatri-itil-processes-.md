<p style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-style: inherit; font-size: 12pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; color: #303030;">The Service Operation phase has the following processes:</span></p><p style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"></p><ul><li><span style="color: #303030; font-size: 12pt; font-family: inherit; font-style: inherit;"><strong>             Event Management</strong></span></li></ul><p style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"></p><p style="padding-left: 60px; font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-style: inherit; font-size: 12pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; color: #303030;">According to Wikipedia, an event may indicate that something is not functioning correctly, leading to an incident being logged. Events may also indicate normal activity, or a need for routine intervention such as changing a tape.</span></p><p style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"></p><p style="padding-left: 60px; font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-style: inherit; font-size: 12pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; color: #303030;">The goal of Event Management is to detect and analyze events and determine the appropriate process for dealing with the events. This can include categorizing opened tickets, automating processes, comparing performance/behavior against Service Level Agreements, and creating the basis of service improvement and reporting. The ServiceNow platform tracks these events in a number of System Logs, and can respond to them in automated ways using specific policies.</span></p><p style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"></p><p style="padding-left: 60px; font-size: 12pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; color: #303030;">Incident management<span style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit;"> is the practice of identifying, analyzing and correcting hazards to prevent a future re-occurrence. According to Wikipedia, t<span style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit;">he goal of Incident Management is to restore normal service operation as quickly as possible following an incident, while minimizing impact to business operations and ensuring quality is maintained.</span></span></span></p><p style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"></p><p style="padding-left: 60px; font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"><span style="font-style: inherit; font-size: 12pt; font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; color: #303030;"><span style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit;">The </span><span style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit;">ServiceNow</span><span style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit;"> platform supports the incident management process with the ability to log incidents, classify according to impact and urgency, assign to appropriate groups, escalate, and manage through to resolution and reporting. <span style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit;">Within the platform, incidents are handled with the task record system. Each incident is generated through a variety of methods as a task record, and populated with the pertinent information in individual fields. These tasks can be assigned to appropriate service desk members, who will deal with the task as appropriate. Once the incident has been properly dealt with, it is closed.</span></span></span></p><p style="font-family: arial, sans-serif; color: #666666;"></p><p style="padding-left: 60px; font-family: arial, sans-serif;
