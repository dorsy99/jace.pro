font-family: 'Source Serif Pro', serif; font-size: 21px; background-position: initial; color: rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.701961);">Five predictions for 2017...</p><ol style="margin-top: 3.2rem; margin-bottom: 3.2rem; font-family: 'Source Serif Pro', serif; font-size: 21px; list-style-position: initial; list-style-image: initial; background-position: initial; color: rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.701961);"><li><em style="font-weight: inherit; font-family: Georgia, 'Source Serif Pro', serif; font-size: 0.975em; background-position: initial;"><strong style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit; font-size: 20.475px; background-position: initial;">Machine learning becomes the new mobile</strong></em>: we founded <a title="chcrunch.com/2011/07/07/bmc-software-acquires-aeroprise-to-enhance-its-mobile-know-how/" href="http://techcrunch.com/2011/07/07/bmc-software-acquires-aeroprise-to-enhance-its-mobile-know-how/" rel="nofollow noopener" style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit; color: #8c68cb; background-position: initial;" target="_blank">Aeroprise</a> in 1999 before smartphones or apps existed because it was obvious that the future of computing wasn't <a title=".wikipedia.org/wiki/Office_Assistant" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Office_Assistant" rel="nofollow noopener" style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit; color: #8c68cb; background-position: initial;" target="_blank">Clippy</a> in a cube. By 2008, iOS and Android ushered in the era of "mobile first". Sundar Pichai officially ended that era with his pronouncement at <a title="w.nytimes.com/2016/12/14/magazine/the-great-ai-awakening.html" href="http://www.nytimes.com/2016/12/14/magazine/the-great-ai-awakening.html" rel="nofollow noopener" style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit; color: #8c68cb; background-position: initial;" target="_blank">the unveiling of an AI-powered Google Translate</a> in November. Google, and eventually all tech stalwarts, will henceforward be "AI first."</li><li><em style="font-weight: inherit; font-family: Georgia, 'Source Serif Pro', serif; font-size: 0.975em; background-position: initial;"><strong style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit; font-size: 20.475px; background-position: initial;">Public clouds make AI OAuth-simple</strong></em>: machine learning leaders Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Facebook, and Baidu have all released machine learning frameworks with APIs that make it as easy to add sentiment analysis or image recognition to apps as it was a few years ago to use OAuth to authenticate across platforms. API platform pioneers like Apigee and Mulesoft created large communities of API-savvy developers. Those developers will embrace new abilities to incorporate machine learning features into apps without dusting off the cover of a Linear Regression textbook.</li><li><em style="font-weight: inherit; font-family: Georgia, 'Source Serif Pro', serif; font-size: 0.975em; background-position: initial;"><strong style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit; font-size: 20.475px; background-position: initial;">Conversation becomes the new interface</strong></em>: Facebook's Messenger and WhatsApp platforms alone process more than 60 billion messages daily. That's an astonishing ten per living human per day. In the enterprise, messaging apps like Slack are creating a new culture of collaboration that is rapidly displacing stodgy, asynchronous email as our preferred mode of sharing. Rumors of the demise of email continue to be premature… but the rise of conversational interfaces in the form of bots and chat-driven automation is real.</li><li><em style="font-weight: inherit; font-family: Georgia, 'Source Serif Pro', serif; font-size: 0.975em; background-position: initial;"><strong style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit; font-size: 20.475px; background-position: initial;">The bots won't take over</strong></em>: domain-specific AI-driven automation will mature in 2017. Cross-domain, or "generalized" AI, won't. For example, IT issues will be addressed by bots that understand how to reset your password or fix your laptop. Just don't expect them to order you coffee or book a flight. Expect neural networks to achieve vaunted "Artificial Generalized Intelligence" status in the next decade. At that point, machines will have the full range of human cognitive abilities. For as much progress as we've made, in 2017 it will continue to be the case that human toddlers will have abilities that far exceed those of machines. For example, both toddlers and machines can learn to distinguish dogs from cars… but only toddlers get excited by a wagging tail. For a great exploration of the topic, read Kurzweil's captivating <a title="w.amazon.com/How-Create-Mind-Thought-Revealed/dp/0143124048" href="http://www.amazon.com/How-Create-Mind-Thought-Revealed/dp/0143124048" rel="nofollow noopener" style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit; color: #8c68cb; background-position: initial;" target="_blank">How to Create a Mind</a>.</li><li><em style="font-weight: inherit; font-family: Georgia, 'Source Serif Pro', serif; font-size: 0.975em; background-position: initial;"><strong style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit; font-size: 20.475px; background-position: initial;">Machine recognition of images, text, and speech will become commonplace</strong></em>: gone are the days when machines identifying faces or translating languages was science fiction. Expect manufacturing environments to use augmented reality to train assembly line workers, smart workspaces that adapt climate, alerts, and apps to your preferences, and legal document review to be conducted via automated text-mining. Machine learning will move beyond the realm of "how" and enter the realm of "why".</li></ol><p style="margin-top: 3.2rem; margin-bottom: 3.2rem; font-family: 'Source Serif Pro', serif; font-size: 21px; background-position: initial; color: rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.701961);">Beyond 2017, expect many AI themes from 2016 to enter the mainstream public conscience. Those include autonomous vehicles (and the regulatory infrastructure to support them), designer genes (and the ethical maturity to make them feasible), and, eventually, <a title=".wikipedia.org/wiki/Technological_singularity" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technological_singularity" rel="nofollow noopener" style="font-style: inherit; font-family: inherit; color: #8c68cb; background-position: initial;" target="_blank">singularity</a> (the convergence of carbon and silicon-based life forms).</p><p style="margin-top: 3.2rem; margin-bottom: 3.2rem; font-family: 'Source Serif Pro', serif; font-size: 21px; background-position: initial; color: rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.701961);">Until then, I encourage you to re-state, amend, or mock my predictions… while I commune with bots and swim with sharks.</p>
