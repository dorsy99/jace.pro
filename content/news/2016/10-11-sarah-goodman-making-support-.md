<p>We've introduced changes to the navigation systems across <span style="text-decoration: line-through;">two</span> <strong>three</strong> of our primary ServiceNow customer support sites. Why? Because we want to make sure that you can easily the <strong>find content</strong> that <strong><em>you need</em>,</strong> <em><strong>when you need it</strong></em>.</p><p></p><p>We are rolling out the new navigation improvements in phases, and in this first phase we focused on three important customer support sources:</p><ul style="list-style-type: disc;"><li><a title="" _jive_internal="true" href="/community?id=community_blog&sys_id=560eea2ddbd0dbc01dcaf3231f961904">Community</a></li><li><a title="i.service-now.com/$knowledge.do?sysparm_type_filter=all&sysparm_kb=124c2ca22bb9f1002f42729fe8da152e&sysparm_category=bfdae4cadb6eee044816f3231f9619fc&browse_type=categories" href="https://hi.service-now.com/$knowledge.do?sysparm_type_filter=all&amp;sysparm_kb=124c2ca22bb9f1002f42729fe8da152e&amp;sysparm_category=bfdae4cadb6eee044816f3231f9619fc&amp;browse_type=categories">Support &amp; Troubleshooting Knowledge Base</a></li><li><a title="i.service-now.com/$knowledge.do?sysparm_type_filter=all&sysparm_kb=a5f38d0b2be931002f42729fe8da1594&sysparm_category=5b5ba8cedb6eee044816f3231f961934&browse_type=categories" href="https://hi.service-now.com/$knowledge.do?sysparm_type_filter=all&amp;sysparm_kb=a5f38d0b2be931002f42729fe8da1594&amp;sysparm_category=5b5ba8cedb6eee044816f3231f961934&amp;browse_type=categories">Known Errors Knowledge Base</a></li></ul><p></p><h2>What's new in the navigation</h2><ul style="list-style-type: disc;"><li><strong>Product focus</strong> — It's what we talk about. It's what we write about. It's what we do. We introduced one product categorization system that reflects our current product catalog. The system uses the same terminology and structure so you know what to expect.</li><li><strong>Category-based navigation</strong> — We implemented a simplified and well-balanced category structure for the navigation. The navigation system groups applications with their respective product category/offering consistently.</li><li><strong>Cleaner navigation </strong>— Categories are organized alphabetically and the number of sub-categories are limited, making the navigation more user-friendly and much easier to scan.</li><li><strong>Behind the scenes logic</strong> — We made sure that all of the old navigation categories are "linked" through a consistent mapping system so content isn't lost and search knows where to look. This means that search will be more relevant and navigation will be easier to understand no matter where you are on our site.</li></ul><p><img   alt="KB blog image.png" class="image-3 jive-image" src="d9af894adb589f048c8ef4621f961935.iix" style="width: 620px; height: 680px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"/></p><p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-size: 10pt;"><strong>Figure 1</strong> </span></p><p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-size: 10pt;">This is an example of the KnowledgeBase Taxonomy-driven navigation system </span></p><p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-size: 10pt;">The Community Taxonomy-driven navigation system will be released any minute ... </span></p><p style="text-align: center;"></p><p>And that's not all! In our next phase, we will be implementing the new navigation system on the Community and working to improve your search experience.
